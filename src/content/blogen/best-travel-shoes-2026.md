Wrong shoes ruin a city trip. Blisters on day two. Feet that give up after four hours. A great dinner you skip because you simply cannot walk anymore.

It does not have to be this way. The right travel shoes carry you all day, look good at a restaurant, and disappear into [carry-on luggage](/en/blog/carry-on-only/). One pair. That is enough.

## What to look for?

Not every shoe is a travel shoe. A few properties really matter.

**Cushioning and comfort** come first. City tours, museums, cobblestone streets. Travelers often cover 15 to 20 kilometers a day. A shoe without a proper midsole turns that into misery.

**Weight** is the second factor. Every gram counts in your [carry-on bag](/en/blog/capsule-wardrobe-carry-on/). Lightweight shoes under 300 grams per piece are ideal. Heavier models are better worn on your feet, not packed.

**Versatility** means: morning at the market, afternoon at the museum, evening at dinner. One shoe that handles all of that saves you a second pair. Cushioned sneakers or clean leather slip-ons are classic all-rounders.

**Breathability** matters a lot in summer and warm destinations. Mesh uppers or perforated leather prevent feet from overheating after two hours of walking.

**Slip-on design** is an underrated factor. At airport security, every second counts. A shoe without laces makes life easier. More on this: [airport hacks that actually save time](/en/blog/airport-hacks/).

## The 7 best travel shoes 2026

The selection covers different types of traveler. From the all-rounder to the hiking shoe.

**Allbirds Tree Runners** are the classic travel shoe. Ultralight, made from eucalyptus fibers, machine washable. They weigh only 244 grams per shoe. Ideal for mild temperatures and long city days.

**On Cloud 5** combines cloud technology with a clean design that works in the evening too. The sole cushions well, the look is minimal. Good for travelers who want to visit a restaurant without packing extra shoes.

**Hoka Clifton** is the workhorse. Maximum cushioning, lighter than you would expect, perfect for travelers with joint issues or very long walking days. Not the most stylish model, but the most comfortable.

**Vionic Walker Classic** is the first choice for those who want stylish leather shoes. Orthopedic insole, classic look. Works with jeans just as well as with a dress. Very popular with travelers over 40.

**Skechers Arch Fit** delivers surprisingly good comfort for the price. Podiatrist-designed insole, lightweight, affordable. For budget travelers, the best option in this roundup.

**Adidas Ultraboost** is the premium running shoe with street style. Boost cushioning, elegant design, still comfortable after a long day. The most expensive model here, but the one that truly fits every situation.

**Merrell Moab Speed** for those who want to hit a trail along the way. Stable, grippy, waterproof in the Gore-Tex version. Not a pure city shoe, but the most flexible option when your trip is varied.

## How many pairs of shoes do you actually need for travel?

The honest answer: fewer than you think. Most travelers pack two to three pairs but only really wear one. One good all-rounder and a light pair of sandals covers 90 percent of all trips.

If you are going [carry-on only](/en/blog/carry-on-only/), commit to a single pair. Lace-free sneakers or elegant loafers are the best choice. They work in almost every situation and save valuable space.

The exception: hiking trips or itineraries with very different activities. A second pair can make sense there. The full breakdown of [what really goes in your bag](/en/blog/travel-packing-list-essentials/) covers this topic in more detail.

## Which shoes work for city trips vs. hiking?

For city trips: comfort plus style. Sneakers like the On Cloud 5 or the Allbirds Tree Runner are the standard choice. Great cushioning, decent look, fits in a carry-on.

For hiking or mixed trips you need more stability. The Merrell Moab Speed is clearly out front there. If mountains are on the agenda, also check [what to wear on a mountain trip](/en/blog/mountain-trip-outfits/).

The rule of thumb: when you are not sure what is ahead, pick the all-rounder. Better slightly over-cushioned than under-gripped.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### What is the best all-round travel shoe in 2026?

The On Cloud 5 and the Allbirds Tree Runner top the all-rounder list. Both are lightweight, comfortable for long days, and good-looking enough for dinner. Allbirds is machine washable. On Cloud has the more polished aesthetic.

### How heavy should a travel shoe be at most?

Ideally under 300 grams per shoe. That makes packing easier and reduces foot fatigue on long walking days. Maximum-cushioning shoes like the Hoka Clifton typically weigh between 280 and 320 grams.

### Which travel shoes are best for foot problems?

The Vionic Walker Classic has a podiatrist-developed insole and is specifically recommended for travelers with plantar fasciitis or overpronation. The Skechers Arch Fit is the more affordable alternative with similar technology.

### Which shoes pack best in a carry-on?

Lightweight, flexible models without steel toe caps or thick rigid soles roll and compress the best. The Allbirds Tree Runner and the Skechers Arch Fit are particularly carry-on-friendly.
