No waiting at the baggage belt. No checked bag that gets lost. No overweight fees. No dragging a suitcase across cobblestones.

Once you travel carry-on only, you never go back.

## What Actually Fits in a 10-Liter Bag

Most low-cost carriers: 40 × 20 × 25 cm, max 10 kg. Sounds tight. It's more than enough.

**Clothes for 7 days (the formula):**
- 5 t-shirts or light tops
- 2 pairs of trousers or 1 + 1 pair of shorts
- 1 fleece or light jacket
- 5 underwear, 3–4 pairs of socks
- 1 evening outfit (if needed)

This works because you wash as you go. Hotels have clotheslines or a handwashing sink. And in Southern Europe, Southeast Asia, Central America — laundry service is everywhere and cheap.

**Shoes: the biggest challenge**

Shoes take space. The solution: one pair that does everything. Good sneakers that work for both day and evening. Flip-flops compress flat and come along.

**Tech: keep it compact**

Charger cables rolled up in a small pouch. Universal adapter if needed. Headphones. Power bank. That's it.

## What to Leave at Home

- Full-size hairdryer (hotels have them; buying one cheaply on arrival beats a bag fee)
- More than 2 books (e-reader or Kindle app)
- "Just in case" clothes (you won't wear them)
- Full-size perfume (travel format is enough)

## The 100ml Rule — Used Smartly

Liquids in carry-on: max 100ml per container, everything in a transparent 1-liter bag. Buy small travel containers and decant. Shampoo, shower gel, sunscreen — you can also buy everything at your destination.

## Which Bags Actually Work

Not every backpack passes every gate check. Recommended models that genuinely fit the dimensions: Osprey Daylite, Cabin Zero, Aer Go Pack. Well-organized inside too.
