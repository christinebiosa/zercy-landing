Pura Vida. Pure life. In Costa Rica that's not a marketing slogan — it's the answer to almost everything. How are you? Pura Vida. Thanks for the wave? Pura Vida. All good? Pura Vida.

Anyone who's been here understands it immediately.

## Why Costa Rica is a must for surfers

Two oceans, one country. Costa Rica has Pacific and Caribbean coastlines — meaning waves for every level, year-round.

**Jaco Beach** is the classic choice. Less than two hours from San José, wide sandy breaks, plenty of surf schools. Perfect for beginners and anyone who wants to be in the water fast.

**Santa Teresa** on the Nicoya Peninsula is different. No mass tourism, dusty roads, wooden restaurants right on the beach. The waves here are more consistent, the vibe more relaxed. People who go once come back.

**Pavones** in the far south is said to have one of the longest left-hand waves in the world. Up to 700 meters of unbroken ride. Experienced surfers only — but those who make it talk about it forever.

## More than just surfing

Costa Rica covers 51,000 square kilometers. A quarter of it is national park or nature reserve.

**Corcovado National Park** on the Osa Peninsula has the highest biodiversity in the country. Jaguars, tapirs, scarlet macaws. No road here leads through tarmac.

**Monteverde** is cloud forest at 1,500 meters elevation. Hanging bridges above the canopy, quetzal birds, clouds drifting through the trees. Completely different from the coast — and just as unforgettable.

And then there are the sloths. In Manuel Antonio they just hang in the trees beside the walking trail. Nobody makes a big deal of it.

## When to go

**December to April** is dry season on the Pacific coast — best weather, best waves, highest prices.

**May to November** is rainy season. Greener, quieter, cheaper. Rain usually means afternoon showers, not constant downpours. Almost always sunny in the mornings.

The Caribbean coast works in reverse: September and October are the best months there.

## Practical info

- **Direct flights** from major European hubs to San José (approx. 11–13 hours)
- **Best base**: Use San José only as a transit point, head straight to the coast
- **Car rental**: Recommended almost everywhere — public transport works, but a car opens everything up
- **Budget**: Costa Rica is mid-range for Central America. Not Mexico prices. Good accommodation costs, but it's worth it.
