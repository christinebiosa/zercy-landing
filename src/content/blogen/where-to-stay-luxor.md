Luxor is the world's largest open-air museum. On both sides of the Nile you will find more temples and tombs than anywhere else on the planet. Sleeping here means waking up surrounded by history. But the neighborhood you choose determines whether you wake up closer to the temples or closer to the pharaoh's tombs.

Here is the breakdown: four areas, who they suit, what they cost.

## Which Neighborhood Fits Your Trip?

**Corniche / East Bank (Luxor Town):** Main city, Luxor Temple right outside, good connectivity, most hotels. For first-timers wanting a central base.

**Karnak Area / North East:** Quieter than the Corniche, Karnak Temple walkable. For temple-focused travelers without city bustle.

**West Bank:** Valley of the Kings, Valley of the Queens, Hatshepsut Temple. Quieter, fewer tourists, cheaper guesthouses. For those focused on ancient Western Thebes.

**Nile Cruise Ship:** No fixed address, a new harbor daily. For groups and comfort travelers.

## Corniche and Luxor Town: What Does It Cost to Stay on the Nile?

The Corniche, Luxor's riverside promenade on the east bank, is the tourist heartbeat. Luxor Temple glows golden at night. Horse-drawn calèche drivers offer rides. The large hotels with Nile views line up here in a row.

**Who belongs here:** First-timers, comfort travelers, anyone who wants to walk to the temple before breakfast.

**Prices per night:** Budget 20-50 USD, Midrange 60-150 USD, Luxury hotels 200-500 USD.

**Top picks:** Sofitel Winter Palace Luxor (Egypt's most legendary hotel, colonial-era building where Churchill and Agatha Christie stayed), Hilton Luxor Resort & Spa (Nile views, pools, contemporary), Steigenberger Nile Palace (upper midrange with excellent location). ## West Bank: How Far Is the Valley of the Kings from Luxor?

Western Thebes, home of Tutankhamun's tomb and 60+ other royal tombs, sits on the opposite bank of the Nile. The ferry takes 10 minutes. Staying here saves time in the morning and lets you begin the day before the crowds pour over from the city.

**Who belongs here:** History enthusiasts, photographers who want the early light, backpackers focused on archaeology.

**Prices:** Guesthouses 15-40 USD, boutique hotels 50-100 USD.

**Top picks:** Al-Moudira Hotel (elegant boutique hotel with gardens, the best on the West Bank), Marsam Hotel (simple, historic, next to the tombs, favorite of archaeologists since 1920), Nour el Gourna Village (pleasant midrange hotel in Gurna).

## Karnak Area: When Is the Best Time to Visit Luxor?

North of the Corniche, between Luxor Temple and Karnak, midrange hotels sit largely ignored by tour groups. The proximity to Karnak (Egypt's largest temple complex) is unbeatable. The atmosphere is quieter than the Corniche. Prices run 15-25% lower than comparable Corniche properties.

October to April is ideal. Temperatures 20-28°C, pleasant for temple visits. Summer (May-September) is brutal: 40-45°C is not unusual. If you must travel in summer: hotel with pool, temples at first light, air conditioning in the middle of the day. December and January are peak season with the most visitors.

**Who belongs here:** Repeat visitors, families, those who want Karnak as their base.

**Prices:** Midrange 50-120 USD.

**Top picks:** Iberotel Luxor (large hotel north of the Corniche, good pool), Jolie Ville Hotel & Resort Kings Island (on a Nile island, peaceful setting), Luxor Hotel (older property, good value for the level).

## Where Should You Book in the End?

For Luxor, the hotel choice is also a logistics choice. The Corniche keeps everything central. [Booking.com](https://www.booking.com) shows Nile view as a filter option. That matters in Luxor: some hotels advertise "Nile proximity" but are several rows back from the water. Read the reviews. For the West Bank: contacting hotels directly often yields better rates than OTAs.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### When is the best time to visit Luxor?

October to April is ideal. Temperatures of 20-28°C make temple visits manageable. Summer (May to September) is brutal: 40-45°C is common. Winter travel (December to February) is peak season with the most visitors but the most comfortable temperatures. The Nile cruise season runs October through April.

### How do I get from Luxor Airport to the hotels?

Luxor Airport is 6 km east of the city. Taxis cost 5-10 USD and take 15-20 minutes. Uber operates and is often cheaper. Most hotels offer pickup service, typically for 10-15 USD. Direct transfers to Nile cruise ship moorings are also standard.

### What does entrance to the Valley of the Kings cost?

Basic entry to the Valley of the Kings is 240 EGP (around 5 USD). That includes three tombs. Tutankhamun's tomb costs extra (300 EGP). The tombs of Ramesses V/VI and Seti I also carry extra charges. A full West Bank day with all major tombs can easily reach 30-50 USD in entrance fees.

### What should you not miss in Luxor?

Karnak Temple (Egypt's largest, evening sound-and-light show), Luxor Temple (stunning at night), Valley of the Kings, Valley of the Queens, Hatshepsut Temple, Colossi of Memnon (free).
