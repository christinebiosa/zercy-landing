A standard cotton towel weighs up to 600 grams and takes hours to dry. For short trips, that might not matter. For carry-on-only travel, it's a real problem.

Microfiber travel towels solve exactly that. They dry in 20 minutes, fold down to the size of a paperback, and barely add weight. Here are the seven best options for 2026.

## What to look for?

Not all microfiber towels are worth buying. The wrong one leaves you frustrated after the first beach day. These are the key things to check.

**Size options:** Most brands offer S through XL. For face and shower use, S (30x60 cm) works fine. For the beach, you need at least L (75x150 cm). M (50x100 cm) is the versatile middle ground that handles both.

**Dry time:** A good travel towel dries in about 20 minutes hanging open. Cheaper models take twice as long. As a benchmark, 300 g/m2 or lighter dries fast. Heavier than that, you wait.

**Absorbency:** Microfiber absorbs through capillary action, which works differently from cotton. It feels drier to the touch faster. Suede texture absorbs more and feels softer. Waffle texture dries faster but absorbs slightly less.

**Antimicrobial treatment:** Essential for longer trips. Treated towels resist odor between washes. Quality brands like Sea to Summit and PackTowl build this in from the start, not as an afterthought.

**Carry pouch:** A towel without a pouch is annoying. The pouch needs ventilation so the towel doesn't mildew when packed damp. A carabiner loop is a bonus for clipping to your bag.

**Suede vs. waffle texture:** Suede feels softer, works better for hair and skin. Waffle is more durable and dries even faster. In humid climates, the difference in dry time is noticeable.

## Microfiber or cotton for travel?

Almost always microfiber. The exception is when you're traveling with a large checked bag and won't be moving between accommodations often.

Cotton feels more familiar and costs less upfront. But it weighs three times more, takes far longer to dry, and in humid climates, it smells fast. On longer trips, that's a genuine problem.

Microfiber costs roughly 15 to 50 euros or dollars depending on the model. It pays for itself across one trip. The [travel packing list](/en/blog/travel-packing-list-essentials/) principle is simple: buy once, use forever. That's especially true for towels.

## Which size do you actually need?

It depends on your main use case.

**Shower and hair only:** M (50x100 cm) is plenty. Small, light, just right.

**Beach and shower combined:** Go for L (75x150 cm) or XL (100x180 cm). The extra bulk is worth it.

**Ultralight backpacking or trekking:** S or XS as a shower-only towel. Every gram counts.

Some travelers carry two towels: S for the shower, L for the beach. That sounds like more gear. Together, both microfiber models often weigh less than a single cotton towel. Pairing them with [quick-dry travel clothing](/en/blog/best-quick-dry-travel-clothing-2026/) completes the system.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### What is the difference between suede and waffle microfiber towels?
Suede microfiber is soft, absorbs well, and feels comfortable against skin. Waffle microfiber has a woven texture, dries even faster, and handles repeated rough use better. For beach and shower use, suede wins. For outdoor and trekking, waffle is the better call.

### How do you wash a microfiber travel towel?
Wash at 30 to 40 degrees without fabric softener. Fabric softener clogs the fibers and permanently destroys absorbency. Skip the dryer and air dry instead. It takes only 20 to 30 minutes. Hand-washing in the sink works perfectly while traveling.

### How often should you wash a travel towel?
With normal use and antimicrobial treatment, 3 to 4 uses is fine. Without treatment, wash after every second use. In tropical climates, more often. Quick hand-washing in the accommodation sink is enough between full washes.

### Which model is worth it for a short city trip?
For short trips, a compact model like the Matador NanoDry or PackTowl Personal in S or M is enough. Light, small, and inexpensive enough to carry as backup to the hotel towel. For carry-on-only travelers, it saves real space.
