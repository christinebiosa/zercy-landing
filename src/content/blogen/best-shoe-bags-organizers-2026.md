Muddy soles next to a clean white shirt. Wet trail shoes pressed against your favourite sweater. Anyone who has packed like this knows: shoe bags are not optional. They belong in every suitcase.

In 2026, the options range from ultralight mesh sacks to waterproof organizer sets. The difference between them determines whether you arrive at your destination with fresh clothes or a ruined mood. Here are the 7 best shoe bags - and everything you need to know before buying.

## What to look for?

Not every shoe bag suits every trip. Four questions help you decide.

**Breathable vs. waterproof.** Mesh bags let air circulate - perfect for running shoes or sandals that are still damp. Waterproof models in PVC or coated nylon protect the rest of your bag from mud and wet beach footwear. For active trips, at least one waterproof bag per pair of outdoor shoes is the smart move.

**Single shoe or pair bags?** Single-shoe pouches give you flexibility: one sneaker here, one sandal there. Pair bags work better for classic travel shoes - less zipping, more order. If you travel [carry-on only](/en/blog/carry-on-only/), single pouches usually win because you can divide the space more freely.

**Zipper vs. drawstring.** Zippers seal tighter - less dust escapes, less odour leaks. Drawstring bags are lighter and often cheaper, but less airtight. For hiking boots after a muddy trail: go with a zipper.

**Size and material.** Shoe bags for sneakers (size EU 43-45 and up) need more room than pouches for ballet flats or flip-flops. Microfibre and polyester wipe clean easily - important if the bag goes back into the suitcase after a few days of use.

## Breathable or waterproof - which do you need?

It depends on your travel style. Mesh bags are right for fresh, clean shoes where airflow matters. Hiking boots after a rainy trail or beach sandals after surfing need waterproof bags, otherwise the whole suitcase becomes a moisture trap.

The practical solution: keep both. A set of mesh bags for everyday shoes, a waterproof bag for the outdoor pair. Done consistently, there are no nasty surprises at the destination when you unpack. The same logic applies to [dry bags for waterproof storage](/en/blog/best-dry-bags-2026/), which work on the same principle.

## How do you pack shoes to save space?

Shoes should never lie flat on top of each other. Instead: sole to sole, then into the bag. This uses the volume better and prevents heels from damaging other items.

Place shoe bags strategically: heavy shoes at the bottom (for rolling suitcases: toward the wheels), light sandals toward the top. Inside the shoes themselves, stuff socks or small cube overflow. ## Frequently Asked Questions

### What is the difference between breathable and waterproof shoe bags?
Breathable mesh bags allow air to circulate and work well for fresh or slightly damp shoes. Waterproof bags made of coated nylon or PVC protect the rest of your luggage from moisture and mud. For outdoor shoes and beach footwear, the waterproof option is the better choice.

### Which size do I need for sneakers from EU size 43 upward?
Look for bags explicitly labelled "large" or "XL". Eagle Creek Pack-It Shoe Sac and Gonex Shoe Bag cover shoe sizes up to EU 47. When in doubt, go a size up - a bag with a little extra room beats one that barely closes.

### How many shoe bags should I take on a trip?
At minimum one bag per pair of shoes. A practical rule: one waterproof bag for outdoor or beach shoes, mesh bags for the rest. For a week-long city trip, two to three bags are usually enough.

### Why are shoe bags better than plastic carrier bags?
Plastic bags tear, trap moisture completely, and encourage odour build-up. Good shoe bags are durable, breathable or waterproof depending on your needs, easy to wipe clean, and add almost no weight. After just a few trips, the investment pays off.
