**Short answer:** No single service wins for everyone. For the best all-round package, pick Welcome Pickups. For solid value, pick Kiwitaxi. For the lowest price and haggling, pick GetTransfer. For the widest choice of options, pick intui.travel.

You land, your phone comes alive, and the question hits: is someone really standing there with a sign and my name on it? Pre-booked airport transfer services promise exactly that. Fixed price, driver at the exit, no taxi queue. Four names keep coming up: Welcome Pickups, Kiwitaxi, GetTransfer and intui.travel. They sound alike but work in completely different ways. Here is the honest comparison, no marketing gloss.

## What actually separates the four services?

The biggest difference is not the price, it is the business model. That drives everything else.

[Welcome Pickups](https://www.welcomepickups.com/) is the premium option. Vetted English-speaking drivers, meet and greet included, a 5-star promise across 360-plus cities. You pay more but get the complete package.

[Kiwitaxi](https://kiwitaxi.com/) is the reliable all-rounder. A fixed price the moment you enter your route, no negotiation, more than 100 countries and thousands of routes. Usually cheaper than Welcome Pickups, service is solid.

[GetTransfer](https://gettransfer.com/) flips the model. You name your destination and the price you want, then multiple drivers send offers. You pick by price, vehicle and rating. Cheap and flexible, but every driver is an independent provider.

[intui.travel](https://intui.travel/) is an aggregator. The platform bundles many local transfer companies and shows their offers side by side, with vehicle photos and a final price. Huge coverage, but quality depends on the local partner you choose.

In short: Welcome Pickups is a driver network, Kiwitaxi is a fixed-price broker, GetTransfer is a bidding marketplace, intui.travel is a price comparison. Four routes to the same goal.

## Which service offers the best fixed price?

Fixed price means the amount is locked before you fly and does not change, no matter how long the ride takes or how bad the traffic gets. No meter, no surprise. This is where they split.

Kiwitaxi does it cleanest. Enter your route, pick a vehicle, see the final price, book. No bidding, no waiting. Welcome Pickups also works on fixed prices but sits higher because of the premium promise. As an example, an Athens airport transfer starts around 40 euros with Welcome Pickups, meet and greet included.

With intui.travel you also get a final price shown, all fees in. The trick is the comparison, you see several partners next to each other and take the cheapest.

GetTransfer is the odd one out. There is no fixed starting price. You set your bid, drivers counter, you decide. This can be well below a fixed price, but it is more variable. If you like to haggle, you save. If you want instant clarity, Kiwitaxi is the better fit. ## How good is the meet and greet service?

Meet and greet means the driver waits in the arrivals hall with a sign and your name, helps with your bags and walks you to the car. At huge airports with several pickup zones, that is worth real money.

Here Welcome Pickups is clearly ahead. The driver stands inside arrivals, speaks your language, tracks your flight in real time and gives you one free hour of waiting. Add water, local tips and a mini-tour to your hotel. That is the reason for the higher price.

Kiwitaxi includes meet and greet as standard too. Driver with a name sign in arrivals, even if your flight is late. Solid, if less built around the full experience.

With intui.travel and GetTransfer it depends on the partner you book. Many offers include meet and greet, but read the description. On GetTransfer you see the driver rating, which helps you judge. Before booking, always check whether the pickup happens inside the hall or just at the curb.

## What happens with cancellation and delays?

This is the part most people notice only when it is too late. Watch the deadline.

Welcome Pickups refunds if you cancel at least 24 hours before pickup. Specifically, you get 80 percent back, or a full-value voucher valid for twelve months that you can transfer to someone else. Under 24 hours, nothing.

Kiwitaxi uses the same 24-hour line. Cancel free up to then, not after. You cancel by email or through the link in your booking confirmation.

With GetTransfer and intui.travel the cancellation rule follows the individual provider or driver. It is shown on each offer, often with free cancellation up to 24 hours before. On aggregators and bidding marketplaces, some user reports mention slow refunds. Pay by card so you keep some leverage if it goes wrong.

Good news across all four: a delayed flight is usually not a problem. Drivers track your flight number and adjust the pickup time. That is the whole advantage over a street taxi.

## Which service for whom?

The decision grid, honest and brief:

- **Business and comfort first:** Welcome Pickups. Fixed English-speaking driver, greeting inside the hall, zero friction. Price is secondary.
- **Family or group with heavy luggage:** Kiwitaxi. Fixed price, van options, predictable, no negotiation after a long flight.
- **Budget and you like staying flexible:** GetTransfer. Set your bid, compare driver offers, save real cash.
- **Rare route or exotic destination:** intui.travel. The widest coverage finds a transfer even where others show nothing.
- **You just want instant clarity:** Kiwitaxi. A fixed price on the spot, done.
- **Driver ratings matter to you:** GetTransfer. You see the profile and rating before you commit.

Rule of thumb: pay for calm, pick Welcome Pickups. Pay for balance, pick Kiwitaxi. Pay as little as possible, pick GetTransfer. Hunt for the right car in a tricky place, pick intui.travel.

## Prices, vehicle classes and coverage at a glance

On vehicle classes the four are close. Standard sedan, comfort, van for groups, often business class and minibus too. Kiwitaxi and intui.travel show the options with passenger and luggage counts before you book, which makes the choice easy. Welcome Pickups keeps it leaner but consistently high quality.

On coverage, intui.travel leads with more than 3,500 airports across 175 countries, followed closely by Kiwitaxi with over 100 countries and thousands of routes. GetTransfer cites around 150 countries. Welcome Pickups is smaller at 360 cities, but deep and consistent there.

By price, roughly cheap to expensive: GetTransfer if you bid well, then Kiwitaxi and intui.travel in the middle, Welcome Pickups on top. The premium markup pays off when you are tired, traveling with kids or dealing with a foreign language. For the bare ride from A to B, the middle tier is plenty. ## Frequently Asked Questions

### Which airport transfer service is the cheapest?

Usually GetTransfer, because drivers bid for your job and you take the lowest offer. For reliably low prices without haggling, Kiwitaxi gives an instant fixed rate. Welcome Pickups sits highest because of its premium service.

### How does the GetTransfer bidding system work?

You enter your route, date and the price you want. Multiple independent drivers then send offers with price, vehicle and rating. You compare and accept the one that fits. The transfer is only locked once you confirm.

### Why is Welcome Pickups more expensive than the others?

Because the price covers more than the ride. Fixed English-speaking driver, greeting with a sign inside arrivals, flight tracking, one free hour of waiting and local tips. You pay for a smooth all-round package, not just the distance.

### What happens to my transfer if my flight is delayed?

With all four services the drivers track your flight number and adjust the pickup time automatically. You normally pay no surcharge for a late landing. That is exactly the advantage over grabbing a taxi at the exit.
