Kids want to be part of the journey. Not just carried along. Their own suitcase changes that dynamic. It gives children a sense of ownership, saves your back, and makes security lines a lot smoother.

The problem: the market is flooded. Ride-on suitcases, spinners, hard shells, soft shells. Some look great but won't survive a Ryanair gate check. This article breaks down what actually matters and highlights the seven best kids carry-on suitcases for 2026.

## What to look for?

Buying a kids carry-on is different from buying adult luggage. Different priorities, different pitfalls.

**Ride-on or pull-along?** Ride-on suitcases like the Trunki are part toy, part luggage. Kids pull each other through the airport. Great for distraction. The downside: limited capacity and an awkward shape for overhead bins. From school age onward, most families switch to a standard spinner. More space, easier to maneuver, simpler to stow.

**Does it actually fit as carry-on?** Most kids' suitcases measure around 45 x 36 x 20 cm, which usually meets budget airline carry-on rules. But always double-check. Ryanair and easyJet have strict size limits. A suitcase that doesn't fit in the overhead bin gets gate-checked for a fee. Verify the dimensions against the airline's website before buying.

**Empty weight matters.** Kids' luggage should weigh under 2 kg empty. Budget airlines count the bag's own weight toward the allowance. A hard shell at 2.5 kg burns most of the allowance before a single shirt goes in.

**Durability.** Kids throw bags. Baggage handlers throw them harder. Robust hard shells or heavy-duty polyester are essential. Cheap construction only shows after the second trip. Brands like Samsonite, Trunki, and Stokke hold up better than no-name supermarket buys.

**Design and buy-in.** A child who loves their suitcase carries it willingly. That sounds trivial but it makes a real difference at 6 a.m. in a departure hall. Let them choose. Dinosaurs, unicorns, or just bright orange. The more they own it, the more they carry it.

## Ride-on or spinner: which is right?

Age is the deciding factor. Kids under 5 love ride-ons. They can be pulled along, they get distracted by the novelty, and the suitcase becomes part of the adventure. That works as long as the child is small enough to sit securely.

From around age 6, the drawbacks outweigh the fun. Ride-ons hold less. They're bulky in overhead bins. And a six-year-old often wants to roll their own bag like the grown-ups. That's the cue to switch to a compact kids' spinner: more space, easier to stow, simpler to navigate.

## Can a kids' carry-on actually go in the cabin?

Usually yes. Most suitcases on this list are sized for carry-on limits. But it depends on the airline. Ryanair, for example, allows children only a small personal bag for free. A spinner needs to be paid for separately, regardless of the child's age.

Always check before flying: what are the rules for children on this airline? Some carriers have separate rules for kids under 2 or don't include a child's bag in the adult allowance. When in doubt, book the bag in advance or use a backpack instead. That saves a lot of stress at the gate. ## Frequently Asked Questions

### What size should a kids' carry-on be?

Most kids' suitcases on this list measure around 45 x 36 x 20 cm, which fits within most airline carry-on limits. That said, every airline has its own rules. Compare the bag's dimensions against the airline's website before buying. Budget carriers like Ryanair, easyJet, and Wizz Air have the tightest restrictions.

### What should a good kids' suitcase weigh empty?

Aim for under 2 kg. The lightest models come in at 1.5-1.7 kg. This matters because the bag's own weight counts toward the allowance. The lighter the bag, the more the child can pack. Hard-shell kids' cases are often lighter than soft-shell models of a similar size.

### How long do kids' trolleys typically last?

Good brands last 3-5 years, sometimes longer. Trunki, Samsonite, and American Tourister have solid build quality. Wheels and zippers are the most common weak points. Brand-name products often come with a warranty or repair service. Supermarket-brand models often last only one season.

### Which features matter most for flights with young children?

Low empty weight, smooth spinner wheels, and a sturdy telescoping handle sized for small hands. For younger kids add ride-on capability. For older kids add a TSA lock and a recognizable design so they can spot their bag quickly at the carousel. A luggage strap or personalisation tag also helps avoid mix-ups.
