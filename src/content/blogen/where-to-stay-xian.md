Xi'an is one of the most compelling cities in China. This is where the Terracotta Army was buried. This is where the ancient Silk Road began. The historic city center is enclosed by one of the best-preserved city walls in the world. Few cities in Asia combine this depth of history with such vibrant present-day life.

Where you stay shapes the entire trip. The historical core is compact and walkable. The outer districts are sprawling and modern. Stay near the city wall and you can reach most landmarks on foot. Stay further out and you'll need to factor in commute time. Here's the breakdown.

## Which neighborhood fits your trip?

Quick overview before diving in:

- **Muslim Quarter / Bell Tower**: First visit, city wall, night market, most central location
- **South Gate / Nanmen Area**: Quieter, cultural, close to center, better value for money
- **High-Tech Zone**: Business travel, modern hotels, convention centers, good metro access
- **Airport Area / Terracotta Army Region**: Early flights, direct access to the Warriors, tourist infrastructure
- **Qujiang**: Peaceful, museums, lakes, fewer crowds, suits longer stays

## Muslim Quarter and Bell Tower: The historic core of Xi'an

The Muslim Quarter around Huimin Street is the beating heart of the city. The Bell Tower stands right next to it. Staying here puts you five minutes from the night market, ten minutes from the city wall, and twenty minutes from the Great Mosque. Hotels in this area sell out fast. Book early.

Price range: 70 to 280 euros per night. Top picks: **Grand Mercure Xi'an on Renmin Square** (upscale mid-range, Bell Tower proximity), **Xi'an Hotel** (historic property, Chinese architectural style, consistently well-rated), **Citadines Bell Tower Xi'an** (apartment-hotel format, great for multi-night stays).

## South Gate / Nanmen: Quieter and culturally rich

The area around the South Gate (Nanmen) is a calmer alternative to the Muslim Quarter. Still very close to the center. This is also where the most popular cycling stretch along the city wall begins. Less tourist buzz, more local rhythm. Good metro connections, lower prices than the Bell Tower area.

Price range: 60 to 220 euros. Top picks: **Sofitel Legend People's Grand Hotel Xi'an** (luxury, elegant atmosphere, prime location), **Courtyard by Marriott Xi'an** (reliable mid-range standard, good for business travelers and families), **Sheraton Xi'an South Hotel** (modern property, well-connected).

## When is the best time to visit Xi'an?

Spring (April to May) and autumn (September to October) are the ideal months. Temperatures between 15 and 22 degrees Celsius. Clear skies, clean air. Summer pushes the thermometer past 35 degrees, with dust and heat making outdoor sightseeing exhausting. Winter is cold and dry, but crowds at major sites drop significantly. The National Holiday at the start of October means extreme crowds, especially at the Terracotta Army. [Tourism of Xi'an](http://www.tourismofxian.com) lists current events and seasonal info.

## High-Tech Zone: Business and modernity

The High-Tech Zone sits in the western part of the city. Glass facades, convention centers, international companies. Not built for sightseeing, but perfect for business trips. Hotels here are more modern with larger rooms. The metro connects to the city center in roughly 30 minutes.

Price range: 80 to 250 euros. Top picks: **InterContinental Xi'an** (one of the top business hotels in the city), **Wyndham Grand Xi'an South** (well-equipped rooms, meeting facilities), **Holiday Inn Xi'an Greenland Center** (practical, reliable, good connectivity).

The deal here: meetings during the day, old city at night. Xi'an makes that combination work. The fact that you can visit the [Giant Wild Goose Pagoda](https://www.dyganta.com) on the side is a bonus few business destinations offer.

## Airport Area and Terracotta Army Region: Practical for early risers

The Terracotta Army is located roughly 40 kilometers east of the city center. Most visitors make it a day trip from downtown. But if you want to be first through the gates before the tour buses arrive, staying near the airport or in Lintong makes sense. It saves time and stress. Also practical: early morning flights or late-night arrivals.

Price range: 50 to 160 euros. Top picks: **Novotel Xi'an Airport** (direct shuttle service, convenient and reliable), **Mercure Xi'an High-Tech Zone** (affordable, good reviews), **Days Hotel & Suites Xi'an** (budget-friendly, airport proximity).

For the transfer to the Terracotta Army: take Metro Line 9 to Fangzhicheng terminus, then Bus 306. About 90 minutes total, very cheap. For more tips on budget travel across Asia, check out [Southeast Asia Budget Travel](/en/blog/southeast-asia-budget-travel).

## Qujiang: Calm and cultural in the south

Qujiang is a newer cultural development zone in the southern part of the city. The Shaanxi History Museum is here. So is the Qujiang Pool, several art galleries, and the Tang Paradise cultural park. Quieter than the center. Less nightlife, more breathing room. Good for travelers who want to explore Xi'an over several days without the crowds.

Price range: 65 to 200 euros. Top picks: **Renaissance Xi'an Hotel** (stylish property, Qujiang atmosphere, pool), **Xi'an Tang Paradise Hotel** (close to the cultural park, historic Chinese aesthetic), **Hampton Inn by Hilton Xi'an Qujiang** (solid mid-range, good value).

Qujiang pairs well with broader Asia itineraries. If you're planning a bigger trip, [Japan 3-Week Route](/en/blog/japan-3-week-route) has plenty of inspiration for building an east Asia loop.

## Where should you book in the end?

First visit: Muslim Quarter or South Gate for short distances and maximum city atmosphere. Business: High-Tech Zone, plan for the metro into the center each evening. Terracotta Army early without the crowds: Airport area or Lintong. Relaxed cultural stay: Qujiang.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Which neighborhood is closest to the Terracotta Army?
The Terracotta Army is about 40 kilometers from the city center. Hotels near the airport or in Lintong are the closest. For most travelers, a day trip via Metro Line 9 and Bus 306 (roughly 90 minutes, very inexpensive) is the more practical option.

### What does an average night in Xi'an cost?
Mid-range hotels run 70 to 150 euros per night. Luxury properties like the Sofitel or InterContinental start at 200 euros. Budget options near the airport or outer districts begin around 50 euros. During peak season (October Golden Week, Chinese New Year) prices rise sharply.

### How do you get from Xi'an Xianyang Airport to the city center?
The Airport Express Bus (Line 2) runs from the airport to the Bell Tower for around 25 yuan and takes about 60 minutes without traffic. A taxi costs 80 to 120 yuan. Since 2024, Metro Line 14 also provides a faster connection into the city.

### What should you not miss in Xi'an?
The Terracotta Army is non-negotiable. Beyond that: an evening walk through the Muslim Quarter with street food, a bike ride along the top of the city wall, and the Shaanxi History Museum (free entry, but reserve tickets in advance). The Giant Wild Goose Pagoda is worth visiting at night for the light show.
