An airport is not a destination. Usually. Then there is Changi. Or Haneda. Places where you almost hope your connection runs a little late. The [Skytrax World Airport Awards](https://www.worldairportawards.com/) rank the best airports on the planet every year. Here is what actually matters in 2026, which airports deliver on their promise, and which European hubs keep disappointing.

If you want to use layovers strategically on your next trip, check the full [Stopover Tourism Guide](/en/blog/stopover-tourism-guide/).

## Why is Singapore Changi the best airport in the world?

Changi holds the Skytrax top spot for best airport in the world year after year. Not because it is enormous. Because it is designed so that time there disappears.

Jewel Changi, the glass-canopied attraction connected to Terminal 1, holds the Rain Vortex. That is a 40-meter indoor waterfall, the tallest in the world, surrounded by a rainforest of 2,000 plant species. There is a butterfly garden with over 1,000 butterflies, a cinema, a rooftop pool and more than 280 restaurants and shops. All of it accessible to transit passengers without any extra fee.

The real highlight: if your layover with Singapore Airlines is five hours or longer, you can leave the airport and explore the city for free. Singapore Airlines and Scoot run an official free stopover tour program that includes shuttle transfers into the city center. One day in one of Asia's most compelling cities, at no added cost. That is stopover tourism at its best.

## Which airports rank second and third in 2026?

**Hamad International, Doha.** Qatar's airport, opened in 2014, is architecturally the most modern in the world. The Urs Fischer "Lamp Bear" sculpture, daylight flooding through glass canopies, unusually low ambient noise. As a transit point for flights connecting Europe to Asia or Africa via Qatar Airways, Hamad is a relevant and genuinely impressive hub. Note: Qatar as a destination is not recommended due to the country's laws that criminalize same-sex relationships. As a pure transit airport without leaving the terminal, it remains a standard connection for millions of travelers on long-haul routes.

**Tokyo Haneda.** Tokyo has two airports. Haneda is the right one. Not Narita. Haneda sits 30 minutes from the city center, has the most punctual departures in the world, and is clean to a degree that surprises first-time visitors. The food courts are actual restaurants: fresh sushi sets, proper ramen shops, quality that you would pay for in a sit-down restaurant elsewhere. Signage is clear. Everything works.

If you are planning Japan, the [48 Hours in Tokyo guide](/en/blog/48-hours-tokyo/) covers the rest. Haneda already gives you a good start.

## Which European airports actually stand out?

Europe has two clear winners.

**Amsterdam Schiphol** operates on a single level. No long escalator runs between separate terminals, no shuttle bus between gates. After landing you can be at the ground-level train connection in ten minutes. The duty-free selection is among the best in Europe. There is even a satellite branch of the Rijksmuseum in the departure hall, showing actual paintings on loan from the museum. A layover in Schiphol tends to pass faster than expected.

**Munich MUC** ranks second in Europe according to Skytrax. What makes MUC stand out: there is a real beer garden inside Terminal 2. Real beer, fresh pretzels, Weißwurst in the morning. In summer, passengers sit outside with their luggage in the sun as if they had wandered into the English Garden. For transit travelers who want a genuinely local impression of Germany, it is an unexpected memory.

Both airports work well for [lounge access without a business ticket](/en/blog/lounge-access-without-business-ticket/). Schiphol has multiple Priority Pass lounges. MUC has the Lufthansa Senator area for premium travelers.

## Why do London Heathrow and Paris CDG keep falling short?

London Heathrow is the most criticized major airport in Western Europe. Five terminals spread across a large site, some connected only by bus. Terminal 3 and Terminal 4 are aging, cramped and poorly ventilated. Average security wait times rank among the highest in Europe. Anyone with a tight connection at Heathrow plans for the worst. And is often right.

Paris Charles de Gaulle follows a similar pattern. Three main terminal complexes, CDG 1 is a 1970s relic, CDG 2 is a labyrinth of separate halls with signage that requires patience to follow, CDG 2E was partly rebuilt after a partial roof collapse in 2004 and still feels rushed. Delay rates there are part of the expected experience. The RER-B train link into Paris works, but the comfort level is low.

If you have a choice, route around both. Amsterdam instead of Heathrow for UK and North America connections. Lyon or Marseille instead of CDG for southern France.

## How do you use stopover tourism as a travel hack?

When comparing [direct flight vs. connecting flight](/en/blog/direct-flight-vs-connecting/), most travelers decide on price alone. There is a second factor: layover quality. Five hours in Changi is not a burden. It is an experience. Five hours in Heathrow is a different story.

The best approach: plan layovers of four to eight hours in Singapore or Tokyo intentionally. Singapore Airlines runs the Free Singapore Tour program officially for transit passengers. ## Frequently Asked Questions

### What makes an airport genuinely great?
A great airport is punctual, easy to navigate, and makes time there feel productive rather than wasted. Skytrax evaluates cleanliness, efficiency, service quality, food options, and transit comfort. Changi and Haneda lead because they consistently score high on all five.

### Which European airports rank highest at Skytrax?
Amsterdam Schiphol and Munich MUC regularly hold the top two spots among European airports. Zurich and Copenhagen follow closely. London Heathrow and Paris CDG rank far below their size and traffic volume would suggest.

### How does Singapore's free stopover program work?
Singapore Airlines and Scoot offer complimentary city tours or shuttle transfers for passengers with layovers of five hours or more. You register via the Singapore Airlines website before your flight. Requirements: ticket on Singapore Airlines, luggage checked through, valid passport.

### Where is a long layover most worthwhile for travelers from Europe?
Singapore is the top choice: five hours gives you time to reach Gardens by the Bay or Chinatown. Tokyo Haneda works well for layovers of six hours or more. Both are significantly better than a long wait at Heathrow or CDG.
