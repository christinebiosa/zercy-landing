**Short answer:** Travel + Leisure named Brazil its 2026 Destination of the Year, the eleventh time it has handed out the title. The reason is not one place but the range: Rio, the Bahia coast, São Paulo, the Amazon and Afro-Brazilian culture. For US and UK travelers, now is the moment because attention is high, connections are strong, and shoulder-season prices are still out there.

In November 2025, Travel + Leisure announced its pick. Anitta on the cover, a full special issue, and a clear signal: 2026 belongs to Brazil. Editor in chief Jacqui Gifford called it a country "of incredible energy and diversity." That is not a lucky guess. It is a call from people who single out one country every year and usually get it right.

So why should you care if you are in New York, Austin or London? Because an award like this rarely stays quiet. It shapes where airlines fly, which hotels open, and how fast prices climb. Plan early and you travel cheaper and calmer.

## Why did Travel + Leisure name Brazil the 2026 Destination of the Year?

The editors have chosen a Destination of the Year for eleven years running. Past winners include Thailand, Costa Rica and Italy. So this is the circle Brazil now sits in. Not the loudest country, but the one most overdue for attention.

According to the official [Travel + Leisure press release](https://www.prnewswire.com/news-releases/travel--leisure-names-brazil-the-2026-destination-of-the-year-302605698.html), the call was about exactly that range. A country that bundles Amazon adventure, coastal calm and big-city culture into one trip. Belém earned a mention for its food scene. Rio for Carnival and music. The Bahia coast for beaches and colonial architecture. São Paulo for art and restaurants. And the quilombo communities for access to Afro-Brazilian history.

That is the real message. Brazil is not one destination but many. You could fly in three times and see a different country each time.

## Why is now the right time to go?

There are three sober reasons, and none of them is hype.

First, the connections. From Miami you fly nonstop to São Paulo or Rio in roughly nine hours, from New York in about ten, and from London in around eleven and a half. American, LATAM, GOL and others serve the routes. Awards like this often pull in more capacity, and more flights eventually mean more choice on price.

Second, the spotlight on the Amazon. Belém hosted the UN climate summit in late 2025, which put the region on the world stage and improved infrastructure in the north. If you want the real Amazon experience, there are more serious operators now than there were a few years ago.

Third, the price timing. Once a country is officially "in," demand rises. Book early in 2026 and you still catch fares from before the wave. We break down how to hit that window in our guide on [when to book flights](/en/blog/when-to-book-flights/).

## Rio de Janeiro: Carnival, beaches and the first impression

Rio is the entry point for most people. And it works. Sugarloaf, the Christ statue on Corcovado, then down to Copacabana and Ipanema. It is postcard stuff, but it is also real.

The next big Carnival lands in February 2027, since the 2026 edition has already passed. If that is the experience you want, plan far ahead. Rooms in Ipanema and Copacabana sell out months earlier, and prices easily triple. Travel outside Carnival and you get the same city at a far calmer pace.

Between the postcards sits the real Rio. The Santa Teresa district with its studios. The Feira de São Cristóvão market. A match at the Maracanã. For guided tours, Sugarloaf tickets or a favela walk with a local guide, [Tiqets](https://www.tiqets.com/) is worth a look, since you book ahead and skip the queues. Where to sleep in each neighborhood is covered in our guide to the [best hotels in Rio de Janeiro](/en/blog/where-to-stay-rio-de-janeiro/).

## What makes the Bahia coast so special?

Bahia is the part of Brazil that Travel + Leisure rightly highlights. This is where Afro-Brazilian culture meets dream beaches. Salvador, the old capital, has the Pelourinho, a colonial quarter full of music, capoeira and churches.

Itacaré is the more relaxed alternative. A surf town with rainforest right behind the sand, small enough to feel unspoiled. And Paraty, technically closer to Rio, is a perfectly preserved colonial town on the water, car-free in the center, with boats out to nearby islands.

The Bahia coast is the reason a Rio-only trip sells Brazil short. Here you slow down. A few days per place, no rushing. For route ideas, see our [Brazil travel guide with the highlights](/en/blog/brazil-travel-guide-highlights/).

## São Paulo, the Amazon and the Afro-Brazilian side

São Paulo is not a postcard, and that is the appeal. It is the country's culinary and artistic capital. The MASP museum, the street art at Beco do Batman, world-class restaurants and a nightlife that never stops. If you are here for culture and food, block out at least two days.

The Amazon is the other extreme. From Manaus or Belém you head into lodges deep in the rainforest. You see pink river dolphins, sleep to a chorus of frogs, and only then grasp how big this country is. One rule: book serious operators with local ties, not the cheapest tour.

Then the quilombos. These are communities founded by descendants of escaped enslaved people. A visit is not a museum, it is a conversation with living history. Travel + Leisure highlights them on purpose, because they show a side of Brazil that classic guidebooks often skip.

## When should you go and what does the flight cost?

For US and UK travelers, the best window is usually April to October. Rio and Bahia are dry and pleasantly warm then. The Brazilian summer from December to March is hot, humid and expensive, but it peaks at Carnival. The Amazon runs on its own logic, where water levels decide. The details sit in our guide on the [best time to visit Brazil](/en/blog/best-time-to-visit-brazil/).

On flights, early and flexible wins. For long-haul out of the US or UK, compare fares across a meta-search like [Aviasales](https://www.aviasales.com/), which shows many airlines at once. Realistically you land around 600 to 900 dollars round trip from the US East Coast in shoulder season, more from the West Coast and around Carnival.

Plan domestic flights at the same time. The distances are huge, and you are not covering Rio to Manaus or Salvador to São Paulo by bus. Internal flights are relatively cheap when you book them early.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Why is Brazil the Destination of the Year 2026?
Travel + Leisure named Brazil in November 2025, in the eleventh edition of the title. The reason is the range of Amazon, coast, big city and Afro-Brazilian culture. Past winners include Thailand, Costa Rica and Italy.

### When is the best time to visit Brazil?
For Rio and the Bahia coast, April to October is ideal, dry and pleasantly warm. The summer from December to March is hot and expensive but offers Carnival. For the Amazon, the best time depends on water levels.
