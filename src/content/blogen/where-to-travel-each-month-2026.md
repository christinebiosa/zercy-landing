Twelve months, twelve perfect destinations. This guide is for you if you're done with random bookings and want to be in the right place at the right time. One standout destination per month, chosen by weather, value, and events.

## Which Destinations Suit Which Season?

Not every place is equally good year-round. Southeast Asia has its monsoon. European summers are expensive and crowded. The trick: travel counter-cyclically. Fewer tourists, better prices, and the weather still works.

**January: Costa Rica**
January marks the start of dry season in Costa Rica. Guanacaste and the Pacific Coast shine. Temperatures around 28°C, barely any rain. A [Costa Rica road trip](/en/blog/costa-rica-road-trip-route/) in January costs from about €80 per day for two, including accommodation and car rental. Perfect for nature lovers and surfers.

**February: Morocco**
February is one of the best months for Morocco. Marrakech sits at a pleasant 18-22°C with rare rainfall. Crowds are manageable. You can do a [Morocco cities trip](/en/blog/morocco-cities-guide/) through Marrakech, Fez, and Chefchaouen for under €60 daily. The snow-capped Atlas Mountains form a dramatic backdrop.

**March: Vietnam**
March is golden time in central Vietnam. Hội An and Hue enjoy dry days and mild 25°C. The north around Hanoi is still cool but pleasant. A [Vietnam 2-week route](/en/blog/vietnam-2-week-route/) in March starts from €50 per day. The rice harvest is on in the Mekong Delta, and the paddies glow a saturated green.

**April: Japan**
April means cherry blossom time in Japan. Tokyo, Kyoto, and Nara burst into pink. Temperatures hover between 15-20°C, ideal for walking. The [Japan Meteorological Corporation](https://www.jma.go.jp/jma/indexe.html) places peak bloom in most years between late March and mid-April. Book accommodation at least three months ahead since April is the most popular time. Budget: from €120 per day.

**May: Greece**
May is the best month for Greece. No mass tourism yet, temperatures hit 22-25°C, and the sea starts warming up. Santorini, the Cyclades, and the mainland are in full bloom. A [Greek islands comparison](/en/blog/greek-islands-comparison/) helps you pick the right stops. Accommodation prices run 30-40% below July levels.

**June: Iceland**
June is midsummer in Iceland. 22 hours of daylight, the legendary midnight sun turns evenings golden. No real darkness, but green landscapes and open highland tracks (F-roads open from mid-June). The [Iceland travel guide](/en/blog/iceland-travel-guide/) covers all the key routes. Budget: from €150 per day.

**July: Croatia**
July means high season in Croatia. Dubrovnik, Split, and the islands are busy, but the sea reaches 26°C. If you want quiet and cheaper, look at the northern Croatian islands like Lošinj or Bosnia's short coastline. Budget per day: €80-120.

**August: Portugal**
August sounds like peak season, but Portugal is more nuanced. Lisbon and the Algarve are packed, yet the north (Porto, Douro Valley) stays calmer. Temperatures in the north reach 25°C, in the south up to 35°C. The [Portugal road trip route](/en/blog/portugal-road-trip-route/) guide helps combine the best spots. Budget: from €70 per day.

**September: Sri Lanka**
September brings northwest monsoon, but it's perfect for Sri Lanka's south and east. Galle, Arugam Bay, and the highlands around Ella are dry and gorgeous. Tourists only return from October. A [Sri Lanka road trip](/en/blog/sri-lanka-road-trip-route/) in September starts from €60 per day, significantly cheaper than peak season.

**October: Mexico**
October is one of the cheapest months for Mexico. The rainy season winds down, and Yucatán and Oaxaca turn golden. Coastal temperatures hit 28°C, Mexico City stays mild at 20-22°C. Día de los Muertos at the end of October and early November makes the trip unforgettable. Budget: from €70 per day.

## When Is the Best Month for Long-Haul?

November and December are especially good for long-haul destinations. European school holidays are over, but Christmas breaks haven't started. Flight prices drop.

**November: Thailand**
November kicks off high season in Thailand. Koh Samui and the Gulf of Thailand emerge fresh after the monsoon. Chiang Mai in the north enjoys pleasant 25°C without humidity. A [Thailand 2-week route](/en/blog/vietnam-2-week-route/) in November starts from €50 per day.

**December: Bali**
Yes, Bali has a rainy season in December. But showers usually come in the afternoon. Ubud in the highlands stays green and cool. Seminyak and Canggu throw legendary New Year's Eve parties. While Christmas in Europe costs a fortune, Bali lets you travel for €60-80 per day.

## What Factors Determine the Perfect Travel Date?

Weather is just one piece. Also check local holidays, peak-season pricing from your home country's airlines, and events. Many people underestimate how much European school breaks affect flight prices worldwide. The smart move: use autumn half-term breaks or fly just after peak summer.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### When is the cheapest month to travel?
September and October are among the cheapest months to travel from Europe. Summer holidays are over, Christmas hasn't started. Flight prices drop by 20-40%, hotel prices often even more. Weather in many Mediterranean destinations is still warm and pleasant.

### Where to travel in winter without a long-haul flight?
The Canary Islands, Cape Verde, and Madeira offer winter sun within 3-5 hours of flying. Cape Verde often wins on value: cheaper than the Caribbean, warmer than southern Europe. All options are covered in the winter sun destinations overview.

### What are the best travel months for Asia?
November through February is ideal for Southeast Asia. It's dry season in Thailand, Vietnam, and Cambodia. For Japan, March to May (cherry blossoms) and October to November (autumn foliage) are the most beautiful and popular times.

### Which destinations offer the best value for money in 2026?
Balkan countries like Albania and Vietnam, Sri Lanka, and Mexico deliver excellent quality for €50-70 per day. Portugal is one of the cheapest European countries with strong infrastructure and outstanding food.
