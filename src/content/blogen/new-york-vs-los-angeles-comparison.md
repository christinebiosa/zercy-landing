New York or Los Angeles. Both iconic. Both expensive. Both unforgettable. But they couldn't be more different.

This is an honest comparison. No fluff. Just the information you need to make the right call for your trip.

## Why is New York the better choice for city travelers?

Manhattan is compact. You actually walk from one attraction to the next. Times Square, Central Park, Brooklyn Bridge, MoMA, the Met: everything is close. The subway runs 24 hours and costs almost nothing. You don't need a car. That's a massive advantage when you have less than 10 days.

New York is also a cultural capital in the truest sense. MoMA is one of the world's most significant museums for modern art. The Met is so large you could spend two full days. Both in one weekend is doable if you start early. Book your [Met Museum](https://www.metmuseum.org/visit) tickets online. Entry is pay-what-you-wish for international visitors.

NYC's restaurant scene is unmatched. Chinatown, Little Italy, Jackson Heights, Williamsburg: you eat the cuisine of the entire world in one city. The [NYC Tourism & Conventions](https://www.nycgo.com) website is worth bookmarking for current events and free attractions by borough.

For where to sleep in the city: [Where to Stay in New York](/en/blog/where-to-stay-new-york) breaks down the best neighborhoods with realistic price ranges.

## When does Los Angeles make more sense?

LA is the exact opposite. The city is massive. Santa Monica to Downtown is 25 kilometers. Without a car you're stuck. Public transport exists, but it's not really built for tourists. That's not a criticism, just a fact you need to plan around.

What you get in return: beaches, sunshine, a different pace. Santa Monica Beach, Venice Beach, Malibu: real West Coast atmosphere. The Hollywood sign, Universal Studios, Disneyland in Anaheim. If you're into film culture or theme parks, LA delivers.

The weather is simply better. Around 300 sunny days per year. New York goes below freezing in January and hits humid heat in August. LA stays pleasant.

LA is also the perfect base for a road trip. The Pacific Coast Highway north toward San Francisco is one of the most beautiful coastal drives anywhere. Our [US West Coast Road Trip](/en/blog/us-west-coast-road-trip) has the full itinerary.

For the full neighborhood picture: [Where to Stay in Los Angeles](/en/blog/where-to-stay-los-angeles) explains the difference between Santa Monica, West Hollywood, and Downtown LA.

The [Discover Los Angeles](https://www.discoverlosangeles.com) official tourism site is a solid resource for neighborhood guides and current events.

## How do flights and costs compare?

Flights to New York from Europe are generally cheaper and more direct. Frankfurt, Munich, Vienna, Zurich: all have direct connections to JFK or Newark. Flight time: around 8 to 9 hours. From 400 euros return is often achievable with early booking.

Los Angeles is farther. Flight time from Germany or Austria: 11 to 13 hours. Direct flights exist, but there are fewer of them. Many routes connect through London or Amsterdam. That adds both time and cost. Budget 500 to 700 euros for a solid deal.

Both cities are expensive. The gap between NYC and LA is smaller than most expect. Downtown hotels cost between 150 and 250 euros per night in both cities for something decent. LA is cheaper for food, especially at beach-side spots and smaller local restaurants. NYC makes up for it by removing the car budget entirely: no rental, no parking fees, no gas, no parking tickets.

## Which city is right for your trip?

**New York is the better pick if you:**
- Have less than 10 days in the USA
- Are into culture: museums, theater, diverse food
- Don't want to rent a car
- Love energy, pace, and urban intensity
- Are visiting the USA for the first time

**Los Angeles is the better pick if you:**
- Want beaches and sunshine
- Have two weeks or more
- Are planning a road trip (PCH toward San Francisco)
- Love film, TV, and pop culture
- Prefer a more relaxed pace

Classic USA trip: fly into New York, spend a week on the East Coast, then take a domestic flight to LA. Flights between NYC and LAX run 100 to 300 euros. That way you get both cities in one trip. Most first-time USA visitors leave happy with this combo.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### What makes New York the right first USA destination?

New York is compact enough to cover a lot of ground in just a few days. Times Square, Central Park, Brooklyn Bridge, MoMA, and the Met are all close together. The subway runs around the clock and is cheap. No car needed. That makes NYC the simpler choice for shorter trips and first-time US visitors.

### Which city is more affordable: New York or Los Angeles?

The difference is smaller than most people expect. Hotel prices are similar in both cities. LA is slightly cheaper for food, especially at casual spots. NYC cancels that out by eliminating car costs entirely: no rental, no parking, no gas. Overall, both cities land at a similar total budget.

### How many days do you need in Los Angeles?

At least 4 to 5 days, ideally a full week. Distances are long and traffic is heavy. You need time for Santa Monica and Venice Beach, Hollywood, and at least one day for Disneyland or Universal Studios. If you want to drive the PCH north, plan for two weeks or more.

### When is the best time to visit both cities?

New York is best from September to November: less heat, beautiful fall colors, fewer summer crowds. Los Angeles is good almost year-round. March through May and September through November are the sweet spots: no extreme summer heat, comfortable temperatures, and less smog than peak summer.
