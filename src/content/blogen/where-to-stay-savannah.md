Savannah, Georgia feels like a city frozen in time. Cobblestone streets, moss-draped live oaks, and antebellum houses clustered around 22 public squares make Savannah one of the most beautiful historic city centers in the United States. The city is compact enough to explore mostly on foot, yet has surprisingly good restaurants, art galleries, and bars for its size.

Where you stay in Savannah shapes your experience directly. The Historic District inns are more expensive, but you're sleeping inside the history. Staying further out saves money but you lose the feel of the city after the tourist crowds thin. This guide walks you through every option.

## Which Neighborhood Fits Which Type of Trip?

Savannah is smaller than most American cities, but the differences between areas are real:

- **Historic District**: The heart of it all. Squares, museums, restaurants, River Street. Everything walkable. The priciest option.
- **Victorian District**: South of Historic District. Quieter, fewer tourists, equally beautiful architecture, lower prices.
- **Midtown / Ardsley Park**: Residential, no tourist pressure, cheaper, needs a car or rideshare.
- **Southside**: Far out. Chain hotels, budget-friendly, but a long way from the atmosphere.
- **Tybee Island**: Beaches, 30 minutes east. Works if you want to combine coast with a city visit.

## What Should You Expect from the Historic District?

The Historic District is why most people come to Savannah. All the famous squares are here (Forsyth Park, Chippewa Square, Monterey Square), the best restaurants and bars, and the riverfront life plays out along the River Street boardwalks.

Who stays here: First-timers, couples, anyone who wants to feel fully immersed in what makes Savannah special without needing a car.

Prices: 150-350 USD for boutique inns, 200-500 USD for premium hotels.

**Top Picks Historic District:**
- **Mansion on Forsyth Park** (mid-range): Right on Forsyth Park, the largest hotel in the Historic District, modern design inside a historic building.
- **Perry Lane Hotel** (boutique): Modern boutique property with a rooftop bar and art collection, one of the best hotels in the city.
- **Kehoe House** (premium): Victorian inn right on Columbia Square. 13 individually decorated rooms, full breakfast included.

## Is the Victorian District Worth Considering?

The Victorian District sits just south of the Historic District, about 10 minutes' walk from Forsyth Park. More locals than tourists, equally beautiful architecture, and prices 20-40% lower than the Historic District.

Who stays here: Return visitors who've already done the tourist circuit, travelers who prefer a local feel, couples looking for quiet.

Prices: 100-250 USD for bed and breakfast inns.

**Top Picks Victorian District:**
- **The Gastonian** (boutique): Two connected 19th-century townhouses, whirlpool rooms, garden, substantial breakfast.
- **Azalea Inn & Villas** (mid-range): Courtyard pool, relaxed atmosphere, hardwood floors and original details.
- **President's Quarters Inn** (mid-range): More affordable inn right on the Historic District border, well-rated breakfast.

## When Is the Best Time to Visit Savannah?

Savannah has a subtropical climate. That affects both comfort and pricing significantly.

**Spring (March-May):** Best time to visit. Azaleas and magnolias in bloom, temperatures around 20-25°C, lively street life. St. Patrick's Day (March 17) is a massive city-wide event: hotels cost more and need to be booked months ahead.

**Summer (June-August):** Hot and humid, over 30°C with high humidity. Fewer tourists, lower prices. If you handle heat and want a deal, summer works.

**Fall (September-October):** Pleasant temperatures, warm evenings, early leaf color. Popular but not as crowded as spring.

**Winter (November-February):** Mild by US standards, 10-15°C. Few tourists, low prices. Savannah in December with Christmas lights is atmospheric.

The official tourism bureau [Visit Savannah](https://www.visitsavannah.com) has current event listings and neighborhood guides.

## Which Neighborhood Is Best for Families?

For families with kids, the Historic District is the easiest choice. Everything is walkable, the squares work as natural play areas, and horse-drawn carriage tours go down well. Riverfront restaurants have wide menus.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### How many days do you need for Savannah?

Two to three days covers the Historic District and main attractions. Four days lets you explore quieter neighborhoods, make a day trip to Tybee Island, and work through the restaurant scene properly. Savannah rewards a slow pace.

### What does a hotel in Savannah cost?

Boutique inns in the Historic District run 150-350 USD per night. Premium hotels are 200-500 USD. The Victorian District is 20-40% cheaper. Chain hotels in Midtown or Southside start around 80-120 USD but without the atmosphere.

### Which squares in Savannah are the most beautiful?

Forsyth Park is the largest and most photogenic. Chippewa Square (the Forrest Gump bench scene), Monterey Square next to the Mercer-Williams House, and Madison Square for quiet and architecture. All 22 squares have their own character.

### Where should you eat in Savannah?

The Grey (inside an Art Deco bus terminal) is the top fine-dining pick. The Olde Pink House for Southern food in a colonial setting. Zunzibar for street food on the river. Collins Quarter for brunch. Reservations at The Grey need to be made weeks ahead.
