Overweight fees at check-in are one of travel's most avoidable expenses. Airlines charge anywhere from $30 to $150 for a few extra kilograms. A decent luggage scale costs less than $12 and pays for itself after one trip.

The concept is straightforward: clip the scale to your bag handle, lift, read the number. Knowing your bag weight before you leave for the airport means you can repack at home instead of scrambling at the check-in desk. Here are the seven best luggage scales for 2026, including who each one suits best.

## What should you look for in a luggage scale?

Accuracy is the top priority. A reliable scale measures to within 10 to 20 grams. That is precise enough for any airline weight limit. Cheaper models can be off by 200 grams or more, which matters a lot when you are two kilos below your limit.

Maximum capacity should be at least 50 kg. Most models hit this. Standard checked baggage allowance is 23 kg on most carriers, but if you travel with a lot of gear or with kids, headroom matters. It is also worth checking our [carry-on only guide](/en/blog/carry-on-only/) for tips on staying under the limit without a scale at all.

Display and usability make a real difference day-to-day. A backlit screen helps in poor light. A hold function locks the reading for a few seconds after you set the bag down, which is almost essential since you cannot look at the display while holding the bag. A tare function lets you zero out a bag you already weighed, useful for comparing packed versus unpacked weight.

Battery type matters less, but CR2032 coin cells are the most common and easiest to find worldwide. Some models use AAA batteries, which last longer but add a bit of bulk.

## How much does a good luggage scale cost?

Between $8 and $20 covers the full range of what is available. Below $8, accuracy and durability tend to suffer. Above $20, you are paying for extras like leather straps or branded cases that add no practical value.

The best value sits between $9 and $12. In that price range you get 10-gram accuracy, a hold function, and solid construction. Occasional travelers can start with the Dr.meter. Regular flyers are better served by the Etekcity or Beurer.

For reference, most airlines publish their baggage weight limits clearly on their booking pages. [IATA provides a useful overview of standard checked baggage policies](https://www.iata.org/en/programs/ops-infra/baggage/) that covers most major carriers worldwide.

## Do you actually need a luggage scale?

Yes, if you fly more than once a year. For a single annual vacation, you can get away with the scale at the airport check-in counter, though that does nothing to reduce the stress of not knowing beforehand. Anyone who flies two or three times a year or more will recoup the scale's cost after a single avoided overweight charge.

Scales are especially useful for budget airline travelers. Carriers like Ryanair and EasyJet weigh carry-on bags consistently, and a few extra kilograms can cost more than the ticket itself. Knowing your exact weight before you leave home removes that risk entirely. ## Frequently Asked Questions

### What accuracy does a luggage scale really need?

10 to 20 grams is sufficient for any airline weight limit. Checked baggage limits are set at 20 or 23 kg, and a gram or two of variance is irrelevant at that scale. What causes problems is a device that is off by 200 grams or more. That kind of error puts you at risk of an overweight charge even when you think you are safely under the limit.

### What is the hold function and why does it matter?

The hold function freezes the weight reading on the display for a few seconds after you set the bag down. This matters because you physically cannot read the display while holding the bag in the air with both hands. Without hold, you need a second person. With hold, you can do it alone. It is a small feature that makes a big practical difference.

### Which batteries do luggage scales use and how long do they last?

Most luggage scales run on a CR2032 coin cell battery. With typical use of two or three weighings per trip, that battery lasts two to three years. Some models use AAA batteries, which have a longer life and are easier to find at airports and corner shops. Carrying a spare in your toiletry bag is a good habit on long trips.

### How small and light are luggage scales?

Between 44 and 100 grams, and small enough to fit in a jacket pocket. The scale takes up less space than a pack of cards. There is no reasonable argument for leaving it at home given how little it weighs and how much a single overweight fee costs.
