Siem Reap is the gateway to Angkor Wat. But where you sleep at the end of the day shapes your trip just as much as the temples themselves. The gap between a backpacker guesthouse and a jungle boutique resort is enormous. So is the distance between neighborhoods.

Here is the honest breakdown: 5 neighborhoods, who fits each one, what it costs.

## Which Neighborhood Fits Your Trip?

Quick orientation first.

**Old Market Area/Pub Street:** The tourist hub. Restaurants, bars, souvenir stalls, tuk-tuks everywhere. For first-timers.

**Angkor Village/Road 60:** Boutique jungle resorts outside the city. Quiet, green, romantic. For couples.

**Sala Kamraeuk:** The luxury zone. Spa resorts, pools, silent side streets. For premium travelers.

**Airport Road:** Budget guesthouses, convenient location. For budget travelers.

**East Bank/Svay Dangkum:** Local restaurants, quiet, almost no tourists. For repeat visitors wanting the real Siem Reap.

## Old Market Area and Pub Street: The Tourist Hub

The heart of tourist Siem Reap. Around Psar Chas (Old Market) and the famous Pub Street, everything clusters together: restaurants covering every cuisine, cheap beer bars, massage salons, night markets, tuk-tuks on standby. Angkor Wat is 20 minutes from here.

**Who fits here:** First-timers, solo travelers, anyone who wants company in the evenings. Ideal if you spend your days at the temples and want a lively base to return to.

**Nightly rates:** Budget guesthouse from 15 USD, mid-range 50-120 USD, boutique 100-200 USD.

**Top picks:** Jaya House River Park (boutique pool hotel, outstanding service), Shadow Angkor Hotel (solid value, central), Mad Monkey Siem Reap (hostel with pool, social atmosphere). ## Angkor Village and Road 60: Jungle Romance for Couples

Outside the city limits, along Road 60 toward Angkor: boutique resorts nestled between palm trees and banana plants. No street noise, no nightlife. Instead: infinity pools, garden breakfasts, on-call tuk-tuks to the temples.

**Who fits here:** Couples, honeymooners, anyone prioritizing peace over centrality. Angkor Wat is 10-15 minutes by tuk-tuk.

**Nightly rates:** Boutique resorts 150-350 USD, luxury resorts 300-600 USD.

**Top picks:** Shinta Mani Angkor (flagship boutique of the region), Amansara (ultra-luxury heritage resort, one of the finest in all of Southeast Asia), La Résidence d'Angkor (classic colonial-style boutique).

## When Is the Best Time to Visit Siem Reap?

November through April is dry season. Good weather, temples accessible without mud. January and February are peak months: higher prices, more visitors. October and November offer a strong balance. The Tonle Sap lake is at its highest after the monsoon, ideal for boat trips. For Angkor photos without crowds: sunrise in March or April, start early. The rainy season (May to October) brings lower hotel prices and lush green landscapes, but expect brief daily showers.

## Sala Kamraeuk: Luxury and Spa

North of the city center, beyond the Siem Reap River: the neighborhood of large resorts. Wide lanes, pool complexes, spa treatments, quiet. If you want to explore Angkor by day and arrive back at a calm resort property in the evening, this is your neighborhood.

**Who fits here:** Premium travelers, anyone wanting to spend significant time at the pool, wellness-focused trips.

**Nightly rates:** Resorts from 200 USD, premium from 400 USD.

**Top picks:** Raffles Grand Hotel d'Angkor (historic colonial luxury hotel, Siem Reap's most iconic property), Anantara Angkor Resort (modern luxury with pool and spa), Park Hyatt Siem Reap (more urban luxury option in a mixed style).

## Airport Road: Budget and Practical

The road between the airport and the city center: packed with small guesthouses and basic hotels. Nothing romantic, but solid infrastructure. Bus connections into the center, plenty of restaurants nearby, short taxi ride to the airport.

**Who fits here:** Budget travelers, backpackers, anyone using Siem Reap purely as a base for Angkor with no other requirements.

**Nightly rates:** Basic guesthouses from 8 USD, solid budget hotels 20-50 USD.

**Top picks:** Angkor Wonder Hostel (affordable, clean), One Stop Hostel (strong reviews in the budget category), Memory Villa Hotel (budget hotel with pool).

## East Bank and Svay Dangkum: Authentic Siem Reap

East of the Siem Reap River lies the neighborhood most tourists never enter. Local restaurants serving Khmer food at local prices. No tourist traps. Quiet lanes, pagodas, markets for residents. If you have been to Siem Reap before and want to go deeper this time, this is where you belong.

**Who fits here:** Repeat visitors, anyone seeking local food and real city life, digital nomads on longer stays.

**Nightly rates:** Guesthouses from 15 USD, boutique hotels 60-120 USD.

**Top picks:** Maison 557 (small boutique hotel with genuine character), Soria Moria Hotel (Belgian-Cambodian concept, excellent in-house kitchen), Phum Baitang (at the city edge, stilted villas, for a memorable stay).

## Frequently Asked Questions

### How much does a hotel in Siem Reap cost per night?

Budget guesthouse from 8-20 USD, mid-range hotel 50-120 USD, boutique resort 120-300 USD, luxury resort like Amansara or Raffles from 400 USD. Siem Reap is affordable by Southeast Asian standards, but prices vary significantly by neighborhood and season.

### Which neighborhood is best for first-timers in Siem Reap?

Old Market Area and Pub Street. Everything is within walking distance: restaurants, bars, tuk-tuks to Angkor. Those preferring quiet and not planning to go out every night should look at Sala Kamraeuk instead.

### How far is Angkor Wat from Siem Reap city center?

From the Old Market/Pub Street area, roughly 6 km, about 20-25 minutes by tuk-tuk. From the jungle resorts on Road 60, sometimes as little as 10 minutes. Almost every hotel offers tuk-tuk transfers for 5-10 USD per day.

### When is the best time to visit Angkor Wat?

For ideal light and smaller crowds: sunrise between 5:30 and 6 am. Main tour groups arrive from 8 am onward. During peak season (December to March) it is at its busiest. October and November are a hidden sweet spot with far fewer visitors.
