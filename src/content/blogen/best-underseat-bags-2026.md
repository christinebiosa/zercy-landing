Flying Ryanair, EasyJet, or Spirit? Then you already know the drill. If your bag fits under the seat in front of you, it counts as a personal item and flies free. No gate fees, no checked luggage surcharges, no last-minute scramble at the boarding door. That is the whole promise of the underseat bag.

Sounds straightforward. In practice, not every bag actually fits under every seat, and fewer still meet the official size limits while still carrying everything you need. This guide breaks down what to look for and picks the seven best underseat bags you can buy in 2026.

## What to Look for in an Underseat Bag?

Size limits are the starting point. Airlines differ more than most travelers expect. Ryanair caps the free personal item at 40 x 20 x 25 cm. EasyJet allows up to 45 x 36 x 20 cm. The safest strategy: choose a bag that fits within the strictest rules you fly by, so it works on every carrier without surprises.

Beyond dimensions, there are a few features that separate a great underseat bag from a frustrating one.

**Laptop sleeve:** A padded, dedicated compartment for your laptop matters for two reasons. It protects the machine and it speeds up security screening. Even better if the sleeve sits at the back, against your body, so you don't have to dig through everything else to reach it.

**Trolley sleeve:** A sleeve on the back of the bag that slides over a rolling suitcase handle. Useful when you're combining an underseat bag with a [carry-on roller](/en/blog/best-carry-on-luggage-2026/) for a longer trip.

**Organization:** Multiple pockets beat one big open compartment every time. Your charger, passport, headphones, and snacks should each have a logical spot. [Packing cubes](/en/blog/best-packing-cubes-2026/) can help organize the main compartment further if needed.

**Material:** Water-resistant nylon is the go-to. Light, durable, easy to wipe clean. Leather looks great but adds weight. For weekend trips this barely matters. For frequent flyers, every gram counts.

**Comfort:** A padded shoulder strap is essential. If this is your only luggage, you'll carry it through airports and cities for hours.

## Which Airlines Allow the Largest Personal Item?

Size allowances vary widely. Southwest and Alaska Airlines are generally the most generous, with no fixed dimensions for personal items. Among European budget carriers, EasyJet allows the most space at 45 x 36 x 20 cm. Ryanair is the strictest at 40 x 20 x 25 cm. Always check the current rules on the airline's official website before you travel, as policies change without much notice.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### What size underseat bag works for Ryanair?

Ryanair's free personal item allowance is 40 x 20 x 25 cm. A purpose-built bag like the Cabin Max Underseat Bag or the Bagsmart Travel Organizer fits within these limits. Always verify the current dimensions directly on the Ryanair website before you fly, as they occasionally update their policy.

### What can you fit in an underseat bag for a weekend trip?

More than most people expect. A change of clothes, a small toiletry bag, a laptop or tablet, a charger, travel documents, and snacks for the flight. Anyone comfortable with a [minimalist packing approach](/en/blog/carry-on-only/) can manage two to three nights with a well-organized underseat bag as their only luggage.

### How is a personal item different from a carry-on bag?

A personal item is the smaller free bag that goes under the seat. A carry-on is the larger item that goes in the overhead bin. Budget airlines like Ryanair, EasyJet, and Spirit usually charge extra for overhead bin access. Only the personal item flies free. If you want to avoid all baggage fees, an underseat bag that fits under the seat is your entire luggage strategy.

### Which underseat bag is best for frequent flyers?

Frequent flyers tend to prioritize durability, organization, and consistent sizing. The Away The Everywhere Bag and the Bagsmart Travel Organizer both hold up well over time and have reliable sizing. The Travelon is worth considering for anyone who travels to higher-risk destinations and wants built-in security features.
