You board in the evening. You lie down. You wake up in a different city. No airport. No hotel for the night. No alarm at 4:30 for the early flight.

Night trains are back in Europe. Not as a novelty, but as a real alternative to flying. ÖBB Nightjet, European Sleeper, and the French Intercités de Nuit have expanded the network massively since 2024. In 2026, sleeper trains connect Vienna to Paris, Zürich to Barcelona, Amsterdam to the Alps.

Here are the routes worth knowing. Real prices, real travel times, and how to book.

## The Best Night Train Routes in 2026

**Vienna to Rome (ÖBB Nightjet, approx. 12 hours)**

The classic. Departs Vienna Hauptbahnhof around 7 PM, arrives Roma Termini the next morning. In between: sleep. Prices start at 29 euros for a seat, 49 euros for a couchette (4 or 6 beds), from 89 euros for a private sleeper. Early bookers get the best fares on [nightjet.com](https://nightjet.com). The newer carriages have USB outlets, reading lights, and lockable compartments.

**Vienna to Paris (ÖBB Nightjet, approx. 14 hours)**

One of the most popular routes since its launch in late 2024. Vienna Hauptbahnhof in the evening, Paris Gare de l'Est in the morning. A few years ago, this trip meant changing trains and cobbling together connections. Now: one train, straight through. Same pricing as Vienna to Rome.

**Amsterdam to Zürich (ÖBB Nightjet)**

Amsterdam Centraal through Germany into Switzerland. Fall asleep in the Netherlands, wake up in the Alps. The route runs via Cologne and Basel. Great for ski trips or using Zürich as a gateway to Switzerland.

**Zürich to Barcelona (ÖBB Nightjet, new 2025/2026)**

One of the most exciting new connections. Zürich via Geneva and Lyon to Barcelona. Previously, this required daytime trains with multiple changes, easily 12 hours. By night train: board, sleep, arrive. Details and booking at [nightjet.com](https://nightjet.com).

**Hamburg to Stockholm (SJ/Snälltåget)**

Scandinavia by sleeper. Hamburg through Copenhagen and Malmö to Stockholm. Swedish operators SJ and Snälltåget run seasonal and year-round services. Prices vary, but early bookers find fares from around 40 euros. Crossing the Öresund Bridge at sunrise is something you remember.

**Paris to Nice (Intercités de Nuit, approx. 10 hours)**

The French overnight from Paris to the Côte d'Azur. Paris Gare d'Austerlitz in the evening, Nice the next morning. Fares from 19 euros for a seat. The trains are older than the Nightjets, but the route is practical and cheap. A solid start for exploring [hidden gems across Europe](/en/blog/hidden-gems-europe).

**Brussels to Vienna (European Sleeper)**

[European Sleeper](https://www.europeansleeper.eu) is a young Dutch company building new overnight routes. The Brussels to Vienna service runs via Antwerp, Amsterdam, and German cities. Book directly on their website.

## The Three Comfort Classes

**Seat car:** The cheapest option. Reclining seats, similar to a long-distance bus. Fine for one night if you sleep easily. From around 29 euros.

**Couchette:** Compartments with 4 or 6 berths. Bedding provided. No luxury, but you lie flat and can sleep. Often the best value. From around 49 euros.

**Sleeper:** Private compartments for 1, 2, or 3 people. Own washbasin, sometimes a private shower. Breakfast included on ÖBB Nightjet. From around 89 euros. The new Nightjet generation feels like a compact hotel room.

## Why Night Trains Are a Double Win

A night train replaces two things at once: transport and accommodation. You save one hotel night (50 to 150 euros depending on the city) and don't lose a travel day. Evening in Vienna, morning in Rome. The full day is yours.

Then there's CO2. A night train from Vienna to Paris produces roughly 95% less than the equivalent flight. Those are the numbers from [Seat61](https://www.seat61.com), the best independent resource for European train travel.

Combine night trains with [cheap flight strategies](/en/blog/cheap-flights-tips) and you get more of Europe for less money.

## How to Book Night Trains in Europe

**Direct with the operator:** [nightjet.com](https://nightjet.com) for ÖBB routes, [europeansleeper.eu](https://www.europeansleeper.eu) for European Sleeper, sncf.com for French services. Direct booking is almost always cheaper than third-party platforms.

**Deutsche Bahn:** Many Nightjet routes are bookable through [bahn.de](https://www.bahn.de), especially for departures from Germany.

**Interrail/Eurail:** Night trains are covered by an [Interrail pass](https://www.interrail.eu), but you need a separate reservation (10 to 50 euros depending on class). Worth it if you're combining multiple routes.

**Early booking rule:** Night trains are popular. The cheapest fares appear 3 to 6 months before departure. Weekends and holidays sell out fast, especially sleeper compartments. Private cabin on a Friday in July? Book very early.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### What does a night train in Europe cost in 2026?

Seats start from 19 to 29 euros, couchettes from 49 euros, private sleepers from 89 euros. Early booking gets you the lowest fares. Last-minute, a sleeper cabin can cost 200 euros or more. The French Intercités de Nuit tends to be cheapest overall.

### How do you book a Nightjet at the best price?

Directly on nightjet.com, 3 to 6 months before departure. The cheapest fares are quota-controlled and sell out. Flexible dates help. Also check bahn.de, which sometimes shows availability that nightjet.com doesn't surface on the first search.

### Which night train routes are new in 2026?

The biggest addition is Zürich to Barcelona via Lyon (ÖBB Nightjet). European Sleeper has expanded from Brussels, and Vienna to Paris now runs more frequently. Hamburg to Stockholm gained additional departures from SJ and Snälltåget.

### How comfortable are modern European sleeper trains?

The newest ÖBB Nightjet carriages (Siemens Viaggio Next Level) are a big upgrade. Sleeper compartments have private showers, USB charging, climate control, and breakfast. Couchettes are solid but basic. Seat cars work for one night. If comfort matters, spend the extra for a sleeper.
