You have been thinking about it for a while. Just leave everything behind, pack a bag, and see the world. Six months, no fixed itinerary, no office on Monday. But where do you actually start?

That is the question most people sit with for way too long. This guide gives you a clear starting point. No overwhelm, no endless checklists. Just the things that actually matter.

## What Does a 6-Month World Trip Actually Cost?

Start with a real number. The range is 8,000 to 18,000 euros, depending on where you go.

Asia is cheap. Thailand, Vietnam, Indonesia: 40 to 60 euros a day covers you well. Australia and New Zealand easily cost double that. Latin America sits somewhere in between.

A rough budget breakdown for 6 months:

- Flights: 1,500 to 3,500 euros
- Accommodation: 2,500 to 5,000 euros
- Food and local transport: 2,000 to 4,500 euros
- Insurance, visas, miscellaneous: 1,000 to 2,000 euros
- Emergency buffer: at least 1,500 euros

Always include the buffer. A last-minute flight home in an emergency alone costs 800 to 1,200 euros.

## When Should You Quit Your Job and How Far in Advance?

Six to twelve months of lead time is realistic. Not because booking flights takes that long, but because you need time to save money, wind down contracts, and mentally prepare for a major life shift.

For most people, handing in notice is the hardest step. Lock in your last working day at least four months before departure. That gives you time to sort out housing, deregister your address, and update your health insurance situation.

One important note: check with your health insurer early. Deregistering from your home country often means losing statutory coverage. If you are considering a [sabbatical instead of quitting](/en/blog/sabbatical-planning-guide/), the rules are different and often more favorable.

## RTW Ticket or Build Your Own Route: Which Wins?

Round-the-world tickets from alliances like OneWorld or Star Alliance sound appealing. One ticket, the whole globe. In practice, they are usually more expensive than booking separate flights and far less flexible.

Zercy tip: [open-jaw tickets](/en/blog/open-jaw-tickets-travel-hack/) are almost always the smarter choice. You fly into one city, travel overland, and fly home from a completely different place. No backtracking to your origin.

In practice: fly to Bangkok, travel through Southeast Asia, fly home from Bali. Or land in Mexico City, travel through Central America, return from Bogota. This setup almost always beats an RTW package on price.

## What Goes in Your Pack and What Stays Home?

15 to 20 kilograms. That is all you need. Really.

Leave the rolling suitcase at home. Cobblestones in Morocco, muddy trails in Nepal, hostel stairs in Buenos Aires: a wheeled bag will make every one of those moments worse. A well-packed backpack keeps you free.

Digital preparation matters just as much:

- Scan all documents and store them in a secure cloud folder
- An unlocked phone that accepts any local SIM card
- A travel credit card with no foreign transaction fees (Wise or Revolut work well)
- Emergency contacts and insurance numbers saved offline

On insurance: this is not the place to cut corners. Long-term travel health insurance is non-negotiable. Providers like [WorldNomads](https://www.worldnomads.com) and specialized long-stay policies are the go-to options. Check the fine print for adventure sports coverage: climbing, diving, and motorbike riding are often excluded by default. ## Frequently Asked Questions About World Trip Planning

### How much money do I need for a 6-month world trip?
Budget between 8,000 and 18,000 euros depending on your route. Asia is significantly cheaper than Australia or New Zealand. Always include an emergency buffer of at least 1,500 euros.

### When should I quit my job to go on a world trip?
Six to twelve months in advance is recommended. You need time to save money, end contracts, and sort out health insurance before you leave.

### What is better for a world trip: RTW ticket or separate flights?
In most cases, individual flights with open-jaw routing are cheaper and more flexible than alliance RTW tickets. Compare both options for your specific route before deciding.

### What travel insurance do I need for a 6-month trip?
Long-term travel health insurance is essential. Providers like WorldNomads offer policies designed for extended trips. Always check whether adventure activities are covered before you buy.
