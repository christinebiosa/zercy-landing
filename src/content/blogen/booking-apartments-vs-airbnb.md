Booking.com is not just hotels anymore. Vacation apartments, private rooms, and guesthouses make up a significant share of its inventory. Airbnb operates in the same market. The question is: which platform is the better choice, and when?

## What do Booking.com and Airbnb each offer?

Booking.com: originally hotels, now with a large non-hotel segment. Booking an apartment through Booking.com has clear advantages: confirmation is often instant, the review system is standardized, and free cancellation is available on many listings.

Airbnb: private hosts, single rooms to whole houses. Stronger on unique properties and residential neighborhood locations. Less standardized, more personal communication with the host.

A quick breakdown:

- **Short city trips (2-4 nights)**: Booking.com is often more practical. Fast booking, no host communication required, flexible cancellation.
- **Longer stays or unique properties**: Airbnb has more distinctive options and local neighborhood settings.
- **Family travel**: Both work well, Booking.com has more apartment-hotel hybrids.
- **Budget**: Often similar, but comparison always pays.

## Which platform is cheaper?

No universal answer. Airbnb charges service fees of around 14% of the booking price plus cleaning fees, which can significantly raise the total. What looks like €60 per night on Airbnb often costs €90 or more after all fees.

Booking.com generally charges no booking fee to the guest. The displayed price is closer to the final price. Cleaning fees sometimes appear, but these are usually shown upfront from the start.

Practical rule: always compare the total price for the full trip, not the per-night price. The difference can be significant.

## Who has better cancellation policies?

[Booking.com](https://www.booking.com) has many listings with free cancellation up to shortly before arrival. This is valuable when plans might change. You can filter directly for "Free cancellation."

Airbnb has host-by-host cancellation policies: "Flexible" with a full refund up to 24 hours before, "Moderate," "Strict," and even more restrictive variants. Many popular rentals have strict policies.

For travelers who need flexibility: Booking.com generally has more accessible cancellation terms. This matters especially if you haven't booked flights yet or have uncertainty in your plans.

## Is Airbnb or Booking.com more reliable?

Both have robust review systems, but they work differently. Airbnb reviews come from guests who communicated personally with the host. Booking.com reviews are also verified and only posted by actual guests.

One advantage of Airbnb: AirCover protection if the property doesn't match its description. Booking.com has similar customer service processes, but the handling can differ.

For first bookings on either platform: set the review filter to 8.5+ (Booking.com) or 4.8+ stars (Airbnb). This significantly reduces quality risk.

## When is each platform the right choice?

Booking.com for most city trips: cleaner booking flow, more transparent pricing, more flexible cancellation. For a genuinely local neighborhood stay, a full villa, or an accommodation with host interaction: Airbnb is often stronger.

For hotels, nothing matches Booking.com in Europe. EU consumer rights for online bookings are explained by the [European Consumer Centre](https://www.evz.de/en).

### Frequently Asked Questions

#### When is Airbnb cheaper than Booking.com?

For longer stays of a week or more and complete apartments. The cleaning fee amortizes over longer trips. For two to three nights, Booking.com is usually cheaper because no extra fees apply.

#### What are the biggest downsides of Airbnb?

Non-transparent fee structure: the cleaning fee and service fee only appear at the end of the booking process. There are also often rigid cancellation policies and no direct comparability with hotel standards.

#### How many apartments does Booking.com have compared to Airbnb?

Booking.com has over 6 million properties including a large vacation rental segment. Airbnb has around 7 million listings, exclusively non-hotel categories. In major cities, both are well represented.

#### Which platform is better for family travel?

Both work well for families. Booking.com has more apart-hotels with hotel infrastructure like reception and housekeeping. Airbnb has more large houses with kitchens and gardens. For urban short trips with children: Booking.com. For rural stays or villas: Airbnb.
