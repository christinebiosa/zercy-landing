The word "dupe" started in the beauty world: a cheaper alternative to a luxury product that works just as well. In 2026, the concept has taken over travel. On Pinterest and TikTok, more and more travelers are actively searching for destination dupes: places that look or feel like famous hotspots but without the queues, the inflated prices, and the selfie-stick chaos.

This isn't about settling. Many of these alternatives are simply better. Here are seven pairs worth considering for your 2026 travels.

## Which Destination Dupes Actually Deliver?

**Instead of Santorini: Paros**

Santorini is gorgeous, and everyone now knows it. In peak July 2025, up to 18,000 cruise passengers arrived daily on the island. A room in Oia for two nights runs 400-600 euros in summer. Paros offers identical Cycladic architecture, crystal water, excellent Greek food, and prices running 30-40% lower. The villages of Naoussa and Parikia are genuinely charming without being overrun with souvenir shops. If you've been to Paros, you understand immediately. For more on the islands, see our [Greek islands comparison](/en/blog/greek-islands-comparison/).

**Instead of Bali: Sri Lanka**

Bali is having a real overtourism moment. Seminyak and Ubud are so crowded in high season that the experience suffers. Sri Lanka offers rice paddies, temples, surf beaches, and lush nature in a more compact geography. Infrastructure has improved massively in recent years. Daily budgets run 30-50 euros per person, well below Bali levels. Add no volcano risk, a rich local cuisine, and a genuinely warm welcome. Our [Sri Lanka road trip route](/en/blog/sri-lanka-road-trip-route/) shows how to plan three weeks perfectly.

**Instead of Venice: Matera**

Venice now charges day visitors an entry fee and limits numbers. It's so photographed that the experience feels oddly unreal, like visiting a set. Matera in Basilicata is a UNESCO World Heritage site, a 9,000-year-old cave city, and one of Italy's most extraordinary urban landscapes. No masses. Hotels carved into the Sassi caves are special, affordable, and unique. It's an hour from Bari, which has direct flights from across Europe.

**Instead of Prague: Bratislava**

Prague is breathtakingly beautiful, which is exactly the problem. The Charles Bridge in July is an airport terminal. Bratislava sits one hour further east, with a well-preserved old town, a castle above the Danube, and dramatically lower prices. Beer from 1.50 euros, solid restaurants for 10-15 euros. Slovak cuisine is underrated. And you can add a day trip to Vienna, which is similarly repriced compared to Prague.

**Instead of Tuscany: Umbria**

San Gimignano and Siena in August: 35 degrees, buses of tourists, and restaurant prices that feel pre-loaded with handling fees. Umbria, Tuscany's neighbor, is greener, quieter, and cheaper. Assisi, Perugia, Orvieto, Lake Trasimeno. Hill towns, wine culture, truffles, and far fewer other visitors. Identical landscape type, about 40% fewer tourists. If you can time it for Perugia Cioccolato in October, that's one of Italy's finest festivals.

**Instead of Marrakech: Fez or Chefchaouen**

Marrakech has changed significantly. The medina is heavily touristic now, taxi pricing is a negotiation battle, and the Instagram spots are so well known they've lost their mystery. Fez is more complex, more authentic, and more impressive. The tanneries, the ancient libraries, the labyrinthine medina feel genuinely different from anywhere else. If you want color: Chefchaouen, the Blue City in the Rif Mountains, is less known but increasingly discovered. Visit before summer. More detail in our [Morocco cities guide](/en/blog/morocco-cities-guide/).

**Instead of Tokyo alone: Tokyo plus Osaka and Kyoto**

Tokyo is non-negotiable. But travelers who limit Tokyo to one week and add Osaka and Kyoto get a much richer picture of Japan. Osaka is cheaper, friendlier, and culinarily brilliant. Kyoto has the temples and gardens. The Shinkansen connects all three in minutes. For Japan planning: [Japan 3-week route](/en/blog/japan-3-week-route/) covers the full itinerary logic.

## Why Do Destination Dupes Work So Well?

The secret is not about compromise. It's about reframing what you actually want. If what draws you to Santorini is the architecture, the sea, and the Greek food: you get all of that on Paros. What you don't get: 5,000 other tourists at the same viewpoint.

Many dupes are also simply earlier stages of what the originals used to be. Paros was Santorini in the 1990s. Matera is what Venice was before mass tourism. Booking now means getting there early.

## Which Destination Dupe Saves You the Most Money?

It depends on your home base and target. For sheer cost difference: Bratislava versus Prague is the clearest win. Beer under two euros, restaurants 40-50% cheaper, and a city center that still delivers. For the most authentic swap: Matera. No entry fee, no queues, and the feeling of discovering somewhere the crowds haven't fully taken over. For the biggest overall upgrade on value: Sri Lanka over Bali, if you prioritize real nature, local food, and genuine encounters over beach clubs and infinity pools. The rule of thumb: the more famous the original, the cheaper and more authentic its dupe tends to be.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### What is a destination dupe?
A destination dupe is a travel destination that resembles a famous or overcrowded place but offers lower prices, fewer tourists, and often a more authentic experience. The term comes from the beauty world where it means a cheaper alternative to a high-end product that performs similarly.

### What is the best Santorini dupe for 2026?
Paros is the most recommended Santorini dupe. It has the same Cycladic architecture, clear blue water, and excellent Greek food but costs 30-40% less and sees a fraction of the crowds. Milos is another strong option with spectacular volcanic rock formations and quieter beaches.

### Why are destination dupes trending so strongly in 2026?
Overtourism has become a genuine problem at many of the world's most famous destinations. Too crowded, too expensive, and too inauthentic. Travelers are actively looking for alternatives that deliver the same emotional experience without the downsides. Pinterest and TikTok searches for "destination dupe" have increased dramatically since 2024.

### How do I find my own destination dupes?
Start with what draws you to a famous destination (architecture, nature, food, climate) and search for lesser-known places with similar characteristics. Good starting points include UNESCO World Heritage lists, national tourism board websites, travel subreddits, or simply opening a map around a famous destination and exploring what's nearby.
