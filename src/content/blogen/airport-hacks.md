Airports are designed to slow you down. Long corridors. Confusing signs. A duty-free shop between you and your gate.

These 7 tricks push back.

## 1. Check In Online — Always

Check in the moment it opens. Usually 24 hours before departure.

You get a better seat. You skip the counter. And if the flight is overbooked, you're protected — last check-ins go first on the list for voluntary bumping.

## 2. Pick the Right Security Lane

The fast lane isn't always the shortest one. Business travelers move quickly — they've done this a hundred times. Families with strollers and shoes to remove: slower.

Watch for 30 seconds before you join a queue.

## 3. Know the Layout Before You Arrive

Google your departure airport. Find the terminal, the gate area, where the lounges are.

Arrivals at an unfamiliar airport at midnight are much smoother when you already know: baggage is Level 1, taxis are exit B, the train is underground.

## 4. Bring an Empty Water Bottle

Fill it after security. Airports sell a 500ml bottle for €4. A refill costs nothing.

Most large airports now have water refill stations airside. Some even have sparkling water.

## 5. Know Your Lounge Options

You don't need business class to access airport lounges.

Priority Pass (comes with many premium credit cards) gets you into hundreds of lounges worldwide. Day passes exist too. And some lounges — particularly in Asia and the Middle East — are simply open to all.

Worth checking before you sit at gate C14 for three hours.

## 6. Pack Your Liquids on Top

100ml containers in a transparent bag. On top of everything in your carry-on.

Security theater or not — it's the rule. The faster you can pull it out and put it back, the faster you're through.

## 7. Eat Before the Airport

Airport food is expensive and mediocre in equal measure. A sandwich that costs €3 outside costs €11 past security.

Eat before you arrive. Bring snacks if it's a long day. Save the airport food budget for a meal at your destination.

---

None of this is revolutionary. But most travelers forget at least two of these every trip. One reminder before you pack is worth it.
