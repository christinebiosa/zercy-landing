Most rental car problems happen before you even leave the parking lot. A dent that was already there. A scratch nobody documented. A tank that's not full.

You're responsible for all of it unless you can prove otherwise.

Here's what to do before you drive off.

## 1. Walk Around the Car — Slowly

Don't just glance at it. Walk around every side. Check the hood, the roof, the bumpers. Look for scratches, dents, chips.

Any damage you find: photograph it. Timestamp on. Multiple angles.

## 2. Check the Existing Damage Report

The rental company should give you a damage form. Compare it to what you actually see on the car. If something is missing from the form, add it — or get staff to confirm it in writing.

No form? Make them give you one.

## 3. Check the Fuel Level

Full tank = return it full. Half tank = return it half.

But write down the exact level. "Half" is vague. Take a photo of the fuel gauge before you leave.

## 4. Test Everything That Moves

Windows up and down. Wipers. Lights — headlights, hazard lights, turn signals. Air conditioning. The trunk.

If something doesn't work, you want to know now — not at the border crossing at 11pm.

## 5. Look for Tire Damage

Flat spots, cracks in the sidewall, uneven wear. These aren't always covered by standard insurance.

And check the spare. Some rentals don't have one — good to know before you need it.

## 6. Read the Insurance

What's the deductible? What's excluded? Glass? Tires? Theft?

Many credit cards offer rental car coverage — but usually only as secondary insurance, and often not for SUVs or premium vehicles. Know what you have before you decline the rental company's coverage.

## 7. Know the GPS Situation

Navigation included or not? Works offline or needs data?

Cheaper option: download Google Maps offline for your destination. It costs nothing and works without a signal.

## 8. Check Your Coverage for Cross-Border Driving

Planning to drive into another country? Many rental agreements prohibit it. Or require an extra fee. Check before you go — not at the border.

## 9. Know Where to Return It

Airport returns, city returns, one-way fees. If you're dropping the car at a different location, confirm the price. One-way fees can be brutal.

## 10. Photograph Everything

Already said it. Worth saying again. Before you drive off: photos of every panel, the fuel gauge, the interior, and the existing damage form.

If a dispute comes up later, your photos win.
