Havana is a city you photograph before you understand what you're seeing. Vintage cars on crumbling colonial streets. Music drifting from every window. Rum and cigar smoke. The clichéd Cuba exists — but beneath it lies a real, living, complex city. Havana isn't perfect and doesn't try to be. There's nowhere else on earth that looks and feels like this.

Here's the honest breakdown for Havana 2026.

## Which Neighborhood Fits Your Trip?

**Habana Vieja:** UNESCO colonial core, plazas, live music. For first-timers.

**Vedado:** Hotels, restaurants, modern city life. For comfort travelers.

**Miramar:** Diplomatic quarter, quiet, elegant. For luxury travelers.

**Centro Habana:** Unfiltered local life, raw, authentic. For adventurers.

**Playa:** Beaches, resorts, distance from the city hustle. For those seeking relaxation.

## Habana Vieja: The Living Museum

Old Havana is a UNESCO World Heritage Site. Four main plazas — Plaza de la Catedral, Plaza de Armas, Plaza Vieja, and Plaza de San Francisco — surrounded by baroque colonial buildings. El Floridita (Hemingway's bar, birthplace of the daiquiri), Bodeguita del Medio (the mojito legend). Restoration work is visible everywhere: some streets are pristine, others still crumbling. That contrast is exactly the point.

**Ideal for:** First-timers, photographers, culture lovers, anyone who wants to experience historical Cuba.

**Prices per night:** Casas Particulares 35-80 EUR, mid-range state hotels 80-180 EUR, luxury 200-500 EUR.

**Top Picks:** Gran Hotel Manzana Kempinski La Habana (Cuba's first five-star hotel, a former 1910 department store, directly on Parque Central), Hotel Saratoga (boutique hotel with a colonial facade, best rooftop pool in the old city), Hotel Santa Isabel (small luxury boutique on Plaza de Armas, deeply historic). Casas Particulares and state hotels bookable on [Booking.com](https://www.booking.com).

The [Cuba Tourism Portal](https://www.cubatravel.cu) has current entry requirements and city information.

## Vedado: Modern Havana

Vedado is Havana's modern city center, built in the early 20th century as a wealthy residential district. La Rampa (the main commercial street), the Malecón (8km oceanfront promenade, the meeting point for habaneros), Hotel Havana Libre (formerly the Hilton, now a state hotel with real history), Plaza de la Revolución (the Che Guevara mural, 1.5 km away). Most international hotels are located here.

**Ideal for:** Comfort travelers, business visitors, anyone wanting modern city life with historical depth.

**Prices:** Casas Particulares 30-70 EUR, hotels 70-180 EUR.

**Top Picks:** Meliá Cohiba Havana (the largest international hotel, waterfront location, pools), Hotel Nacional de Cuba (Hemingway's second home, gangster history, iconic garden terrace with sea views), Hotel Habana Libre (historically significant, functional).

## Miramar: Elegance and Quiet

Miramar was Havana's wealthiest neighborhood before the revolution. Today it houses embassies, international organizations, and quiet streets lined with Art Nouveau and Art Deco villas. Far from the bustle of the old city — less interesting for short visits, ideal for longer stays. The Estadio Latinoamericano (baseball!) is within walking distance.

**Ideal for:** Luxury travelers, long-stay visitors, anyone who wants calm combined with elegance.

**Prices:** Luxury 120-350 EUR, Casas Particulares 40-90 EUR.

**Top Picks:** El Comodoro (quiet villa complex, pool, beach nearby), hotels near the Miramar Trade Center (several business hotels with international standards).

## Wann ist die beste Reisezeit für Havanna?

December to April is Havana's dry season. Temperatures sit between 22-28°C with no humidity. High season means higher prices, but the weather is consistently the best of the year.

May to November brings the rainy season, possible tropical storms (September and October are most active), and temperatures between 28-35°C with high humidity. Prices drop significantly and there are fewer tourists.

New Year's Eve and Carnival (February/March): Havana at its most festive — but hotels are fully booked months in advance and prices spike accordingly.

## Centro Habana: Unfiltered Cuba

Centro Habana is unglamorous Cuba. Market scenes, peeling facades, bicycles and coco-taxis, street music through open windows. The neighborhood between Habana Vieja and Vedado is not for comfort tourists — but it's the most honest version of the city. Almost no hotels here, mainly Casas Particulares.

**Ideal for:** Experienced travelers, photographers, anyone who wants Cuba beyond the polished surface.

**Prices:** Casas Particulares 25-60 EUR.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### What visa do German and EU citizens need for Cuba?

EU citizens need a Tourist Card (Tarjeta del Turista) instead of a full visa. It costs around 25 EUR and can be obtained online or at the airport. Travel health insurance is mandatory for entry. Stay duration: max 30 days, extendable to 90 days while in Cuba. Your passport must be valid for at least 6 months.

### How safe is Havana for tourists?

Havana is very safe by Latin American capital standards. Petty crime (pickpocketing in tourist areas) is possible, but violent crime is rare. An evening walk along the Malecón is perfectly normal for habaneros. Standard precautions apply: don't display valuables, avoid unfamiliar shortcuts at night.

### Which classic car tour is worth doing in Havana?

The classic Havana convertible tour (1 hour, around 30-50 USD) covers the Malecón, Vedado, Habana Vieja, and the Plaza de la Revolución. Book with two people and negotiate directly with the driver — skip the tour operator markup. A day trip to Varadero (2 hours, road conditions vary) is recommended for beach lovers but not essential.

### What is the Malecón and why does everyone talk about it?

The Malecón is Havana's most famous promenade: 8km along the northern coast from Habana Vieja through Centro Habana to Vedado. Free, always open. In the evenings it becomes the gathering point for the whole city: fishermen, couples, musicians, teenagers. During storms, waves crash directly over the road. A Malecón walk at sunset is the best free experience Havana offers.
