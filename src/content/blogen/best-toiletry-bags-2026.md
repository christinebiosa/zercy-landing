A toiletry bag is the least glamorous piece of travel gear you own. Until you don't have a good one. If you've ever dug through a flat zip pouch looking for your toothbrush at 6 a.m. in a dim hotel bathroom, you know the feeling. A great toiletry bag hangs on a hook, keeps everything visible, and has a separate wet pocket. Simple things that save time every single day on the road.

This guide is for travelers who are done making do. Seven real products, one clear buying guide. Weekend trips, long hauls, carry-on-only setups, all covered.

## What to look for?

The first decision: hanging or flat. Hanging toiletry bags have a hook or carabiner and can be suspended from any towel bar, door handle, or shower rail. That matters in small hotel bathrooms, hostels, and campsite showers. Flat dopp kits are lighter and slide easily into tight packing cubes, but you end up rearranging them every morning.

A wet pocket or water-resistant lining is non-negotiable. Travel-size bottles leak. Always. A sealed separate compartment for damp items protects everything else. If you're [traveling carry-on only](/en/blog/carry-on-only/), you'll also want a TSA-clear option: a transparent zip pouch for liquids either built in or included. The 100 ml / 3.4 oz rule applies to all liquids in carry-on luggage, and everything must fit into a single 1-liter transparent bag. For material, coated nylon or EVA wipes clean and handles humidity far better than fabric. Size: 2-3 liters works for weekends, 4-6 liters is right for longer trips.

## Hanging vs. flat: which one is right for you?

Hanging bags win almost every time once you're traveling beyond boutique hotels with marble countertops. Hostels have zero counter space. Budget hotels often have one small shelf. Campsites have none. Hang the bag, unzip, grab what you need. Done. Flat bags make sense as a second, lighter option: for weekend trips when you only bring a handful of items, or as the liquid-only pouch you drop into your clear security bag. The best setup for frequent travelers: one light flat pouch for the 100 ml liquids, one small hanging bag for everything else.

## What goes in a carry-on toiletry bag?

The liquids rule is clear: all liquids, gels, and creams must be in containers of 100 ml or less, and all containers must fit inside a single transparent zip-lock bag of no more than 1 liter. One bag per person. You take it out at the security checkpoint. Solid cosmetics like shampoo bars, conditioner bars, and soap are not classified as liquids, which makes them the carry-on traveler's best friend. ## Frequently Asked Questions

### What size toiletry bag do I need for travel?

For weekend trips, 2-3 liters is enough. For longer trips or if you use a lot of skincare products, 4-6 liters makes more sense. Anything over 6 liters starts to feel heavy in a carry-on and can be hard to fit alongside other gear.

### What material is best for a travel toiletry bag?

Coated nylon and EVA plastic wipe clean and dry fast. Fabric looks nice but absorbs moisture. For anyone who travels regularly, water-resistant material is the clear winner.

### How heavy should a toiletry bag be?

As light as possible. Good models weigh between 100 and 300 grams empty. Every gram counts when you're pushing the limits of a carry-on weight allowance.

### Which liquids are allowed in a carry-on toiletry bag?

All liquids, gels, creams, and pastes must be in containers of 100 ml or less and fit together in a single 1-liter transparent zip bag. Solid alternatives like shampoo bars are exempt from this rule and don't count toward the limit.
