**Short answer:** Panama is one of the most attractive places to move to in 2026. In the InterNations Expat Insider 2025 survey it ranked number one overall for the second year running, and it was the only country to top all five categories at once. Retirees have the generous Pensionado visa, while workers and investors have the Friendly Nations Visa and the Qualified Investor Visa. The cost of living runs about a third lower than in the US, everything is priced in US dollars, and the climate stays warm year round.

For many Americans and Brits, Panama used to be just a canal on the map. That is changing fast. A stable dollar economy, a clear retirement visa and low living costs make it a serious option for retirement or a fresh start, all within easy reach of the US. This guide covers the three main visas, the real costs, and where expats actually live.

## Why does Panama top the rankings in 2026?

The annual InterNations Expat Insider is the largest survey of expats worldwide. In the 2025 edition, Panama ranked first out of 46 countries, and it did so across all five sub-indices at the same time: Working Abroad, Expat Essentials, Ease of Settling In, Personal Finance and Quality of Life. No other country managed that. Satisfaction is striking too: 94 percent of respondents in Panama said they were happy with life there, against a global average of 68 percent.

Panama draws retirees in particular. More than a quarter of expats in the country are already retired, compared with around eleven percent globally. The reasons are practical. The official language is Spanish, and the official currency is the Balboa, which is pegged one to one to the US dollar and replaced in daily life by US dollar bills. You can read more background on the country at [Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Panama). The climate is tropical and warm all year, with no real winter. Tocumen airport also gives you direct flights across most of the Americas, so home is never far.

## How does the Pensionado visa work for retirees?

The Pensionado visa is the most famous retirement visa in the world, and the main reason so many expats choose Panama. You need a lifetime pension of at least 1,000 US dollars a month from a reliable source, such as Social Security, a government or military pension, a company plan or a private annuity. If you own property in Panama worth over 100,000 US dollars, the threshold drops to 750 US dollars. Each accompanying dependent adds roughly 250 US dollars.

The big advantage is speed. You get permanent residency straight away, with no multi-year waiting period. After that you only need to spend a single day in Panama each calendar year to keep the status. Processing usually takes four to six months.

Then there are the famous discounts under Panama's retiree law. Depending on the category, they run from 20 to 50 percent, on things like medical bills, medicines, flights, hotels, restaurants, public transport and entertainment. A one-time duty-free import of household goods is included too. These benefits last for life and make a real difference to a monthly budget.

## What changed with the Friendly Nations Visa?

The Friendly Nations Visa is aimed at professionals and investors from around 50 friendly countries, including the US, Canada, the UK, Australia and most of the EU. One thing to know upfront: the program was overhauled in 2021. The old route of proving vague economic activity plus a small bank deposit is gone.

Today you must show a real, verifiable economic tie to Panama through one of three paths: a job offer from a Panamanian company with registration in the social security system, a real estate purchase worth at least 200,000 US dollars, or a fixed bank deposit of at least 200,000 US dollars in a Panamanian bank. There is also no more instant permanent residency. Instead you get a two-year provisional residency first, then apply separately for the permanent one. The first stage typically takes four to six months.

## Qualified Investor Visa: the fast track

If you are bringing capital and want no waiting period, look at the Qualified Investor Visa. It grants permanent residency immediately, with no provisional stage, and approval can come in around 90 days.

The most common route is a real estate investment of at least 300,000 US dollars right now. Alternatives are a stock market investment of at least 500,000 US dollars through a licensed Panamanian broker, or a fixed-term bank deposit of at least 750,000 US dollars held for five years. One timing note matters in 2026: the 300,000-dollar real estate threshold only applies until 15 October 2026. After that, the minimum rises permanently to 500,000 US dollars. If this is your route, keep that date in view.

## What does living in Panama really cost?

The short version is that it costs far less than the US or Western Europe. For a single person, the cost of living runs about a third below US levels. In smaller towns and rural areas you can live comfortably on roughly 800 to 1,500 US dollars a month. Panama City costs more, up to around 2,500 US dollars for a comfortable lifestyle with rent in a good neighborhood.

Fresh produce and local food are cheap. Imported goods and electronics are where prices climb. A major plus is the currency itself. Because everything runs in US dollars, there is no exchange-rate risk, and savings held in dollars keep their value more predictably than in countries with their own volatile currency. ## Panama City, Boquete or Coronado? The location comparison

No single spot suits every lifestyle. These three regions cover most expat profiles.

**Panama City** is the urban choice. A skyline, a modern metro, international restaurants and excellent private hospitals, with Hospital Punta Pacifica among the best known. Ideal if you want city life, connections and infrastructure. In return, rent and daily costs are higher.

**Boquete** sits in the cool western highlands, near the Costa Rica border. Spring-like weather instead of tropical heat, lush greenery, coffee farms and a large, established community of international retirees. Perfect for anyone seeking calm, nature and mild temperatures.

**Coronado** is the Pacific beach town, about an hour by car from the capital. A well-developed expat scene, good shopping and closeness to the city without living in the middle of it. Ideal for sun and sea lovers who still want a hospital nearby.

A smart middle path is to rent for a few months and try two or three regions before you buy. If you are weighing countries, the [Costa Rica vs. Panama](/en/blog/costa-rica-vs-panama/) comparison lays out an honest side by side.

## Taxes, healthcare and the practical side before you move

Panama taxes territorially. In plain terms, only income earned inside Panama is taxable there. Your foreign pension and investment income from abroad are generally not taxed in Panama. How your home-country rules apply is best checked with a tax adviser in advance, because the US taxes citizens on worldwide income and other countries may still have claims.

Healthcare in Panama City and the larger centers is good, with modern private clinics that are affordable by US standards. ## Frequently Asked Questions

### How much pension do I need for the Pensionado visa?
You need a lifetime pension of at least 1,000 US dollars a month from a reliable source. If you own property in Panama worth over 100,000 US dollars, 750 US dollars is enough. Each accompanying dependent adds roughly 250 US dollars a month.

### What language do people speak in Panama?
The official language is Spanish. In Panama City and expat areas like Boquete and Coronado you can often get by in English too, especially in clinics and business settings. For everyday life and government offices, some basic Spanish helps a lot.

### Where do most expats live in Panama?
The largest international communities are in the Boquete highlands, the Coronado beach area and Panama City. Boquete is especially popular with retirees for its mild climate, Coronado draws beach lovers, and the city offers the most infrastructure and healthcare.

### How long does it take to get residency?
The Pensionado visa and the first stage of the Friendly Nations Visa usually take four to six months. The Qualified Investor Visa is faster, with permanent residency possible in around 90 days.
