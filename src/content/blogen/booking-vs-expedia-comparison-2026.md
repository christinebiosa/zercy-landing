You open Booking.com, Expedia, and Hotels.com at the same time. Same hotel, same dates. The prices are nearly identical. That's not a coincidence.

It's called rate parity. Hotels sign contracts committing to offer the same base price across all platforms. Cheaper prices on the hotel's own website? Officially prohibited in many markets. And yet real differences do exist. They're just harder to spot.

## Why are prices the same everywhere, and where do actual differences come from?

Rate parity applies to the base room price. What sets platforms apart are loyalty programs, member-only rates, and cancellation flexibility.

Booking Genius (free from level 1, better from level 2 after a few bookings) gives you up to 15 percent off on participating hotels. Not every property. But a lot of them. If you book through Booking regularly, the account pays for itself quickly.

Expedia One Key combines hotels, flights, and rental cars into a single points system. You earn OneKeyCash and can apply it to the next booking. This makes Expedia especially attractive when you book flight and hotel together.

Hotels.com has the most straightforward model: book 10 nights, get 1 night free. Simple, transparent, and useful if you travel frequently.

## When does each platform win?

Here's the honest breakdown:

| Situation | Best choice |
|---|---|
| Flexible cancellation, short trip | Booking.com (free cancellation options widely available) |
| Flight + hotel together | Expedia (One Key points on both) |
| Frequent traveler, many nights | Hotels.com (free night reward) |
| Comparing flight prices | Kayak or [Google Flights](/en/blog/google-flights-tips-tricks-2026) |
| Apartments or private rentals | Booking.com (large inventory) or Airbnb |

One important clarification: Kayak is not a booking platform. It's a metasearch engine. You see prices but get redirected to other sites to actually book. Great for comparing. Not useful for earning loyalty points.

Google Flights works the same way. Compare prices there, then book directly with the airline or an OTA.

## When does booking directly with the hotel make sense?

The short answer: rarely, but in specific situations it does.

Hotels have real motivation to win you back from Booking and Expedia. They typically pay 15 to 25 percent commission to OTAs. That means direct bookings are more profitable for them, and some share that margin with guests.

Calling the hotel directly can pay off when you want a specific room type (ocean view, top floor, quiet side), when you're booking a small boutique hotel without rate parity contracts, or when you're staying for multiple weeks and want to negotiate a weekly rate.

Classic direct booking perks: free breakfast, early check-in, late checkout, room category upgrade. None of that is guaranteed through Booking. For a full breakdown, see our [Booking vs. direct booking comparison](/en/blog/booking-vs-direct-hotel-booking).

That said: for most standard bookings, Booking.com and Expedia offer more security through buyer protection, better cancellation policies, and a complete overview of alternatives. Direct booking is the exception, not the default.

## Why do cancellation terms matter when choosing a platform?

Booking.com leads here by a clear margin.

The share of hotels with free cancellation up to 24 or 48 hours before arrival is higher on Booking than on Expedia. Expedia has flexible rates too, but they're less prominently displayed. Hotels.com sometimes shows cancellation costs only at checkout.

Practical tip: always filter for "Free cancellation" even if you don't plan to cancel. It gives you room to maneuver if plans change.

For long stays of several weeks or months, apartments become a separate category. Airbnb earns points here for kitchen access and living space. But Booking.com has a large apartment inventory too. Our [Airbnb vs hotel comparison](/en/blog/airbnb-vs-hotel-comparison) explains when each accommodation type fits best.

## How do you find the cheapest flights?

Flights are a different category from hotels.

Both Expedia and Booking.com offer flights. But the price difference compared to booking directly with the airline is often minimal, and OTA cancellation policies for flights are noticeably worse than for hotels.

Better approach: use Google Flights and Kayak to scan prices, then book directly with the airline. Why directly? Because flight changes, cancellations, and refunds go much more smoothly when you're dealing with the airline rather than an intermediary.

Google Flights also shows price history for the past weeks. Useful when you want to know if the current price is good or if it might drop. More on that in our [Google Flights tricks guide](/en/blog/google-flights-tips-tricks-2026).

## Frequently Asked Questions

### What is rate parity in hotel booking?

Rate parity is a contractual agreement between hotels and booking platforms. The hotel cannot offer the same room type cheaper on any platform than on Booking.com or Expedia. Lower direct prices on the hotel's own website are therefore often restricted or prohibited, depending on the country and contract terms.

### Which loyalty program saves the most money?

For hotel-only bookings: Booking Genius from level 2 onward (roughly 5 bookings) with up to 15 percent off on many properties. For combined flight and hotel trips: Expedia One Key, because you earn points on both. Hotels.com Rewards makes sense if you book very frequently and prioritize the free night perk.

### When is booking directly with the hotel better than Booking.com?

Direct booking makes sense at boutique hotels without rate parity contracts, for long stays where you can negotiate a special rate, or when you want specific extras like a particular room, free breakfast, or an upgrade. For most standard bookings, Booking.com offers more flexibility and customer protection.

### How do you compare flight prices most effectively?

Google Flights and Kayak show prices from many providers at once. Both are metasearch engines, not booking platforms. For the actual booking, going directly to the airline gives you better service options if anything changes. Expedia and Booking.com work well for package deals (flight plus hotel), less so for pure flight price optimization.
