Sometimes flight prices appear that simply cannot be right. Business class to Singapore for $400. Economy to New York for $90. These are error fares, also called mistake fares. They happen because of data glitches, currency conversion errors, or human input mistakes when airlines load tariffs into booking systems. And they are legally bookable, even if not always guaranteed.

Move fast and know the right sources, and you can save hundreds or even thousands of dollars on a single trip. The risk: airlines can cancel the booking. Here's how that works, what rules apply, and how to protect yourself.

## Where do error fares appear first?

Error fares spread quickly because online travel agencies and metasearch engines pull tariffs automatically. Mistakes usually originate directly in the airline's pricing system (GDS) and land simultaneously on platforms like [Google Flights](https://www.google.com/flights), Kayak, and Skyscanner.

The best sources for catching error fares early:

- **Secret Flying** (secretflying.com): The most well-known platform for error fares and deals. Updated daily, with direct booking links.
- **Scott's Cheap Flights / Going.com**: Especially strong for North America. Premium plan includes exclusive error fare alerts.
- **The Flight Deal** (theflightdeal.com): Focused on US-departing routes, plus international deals.
- **Reddit r/churning and r/flights**: Very fast community. Error fares often get posted there first.
- **Jack's Flight Club**: European focus, popular in the UK and Germany.

Follow at least two or three of these sources. Secret Flying often covers the essentials, but Reddit sometimes surfaces fares that appear nowhere else.

## How do you set up alert tools for cheap flights?

Manual searching takes time. Better to set up price alerts that notify you automatically.

The key tools:

**Google Flights price alerts** are free and reliable for standard routes. Set an alert on a route and Google emails you when the price drops. For error fares, though, often too slow. By the time the alert arrives, the deal is gone.

**Kayak Price Alerts** work similarly. Useful as a supplement, not as a primary source for mistake fares.

**Scott's Cheap Flights (Going.com)** is the most reliable paid service (from around $30/year). The team manually vets deals before sending them out. False alarms are rare.

**Airfarewatchdog** is another useful tracker, especially for specific departure airports.

For serious error fare hunting, though, you need more than alerts. You need to be active. Join Telegram groups or Discord servers from flight deal communities. Notifications go out in minutes there.

One thing to know: many deals expire the moment the airline notices. That can be 30 minutes or 12 hours. Speed is everything.

## How do you book an error fare correctly and fast?

Seeing an error fare does not mean you have it. Between the deal alert and a confirmed booking, there are often just minutes.

**Step 1: Open immediately, don't deliberate.** If a fare interests you, go to the booking page directly. Don't wait for friends, don't compare options first.

**Step 2: Pay with a credit card.** Credit cards offer chargeback protection. If the airline cancels and no refund comes, you can dispute the transaction.

**Step 3: Don't add extras yet.** Seat selection, luggage, meals booked separately increases the risk of losing money if the booking gets cancelled. Add them after confirmation.

**Step 4: Wait for the confirmation.** A real booking confirmation (not just a payment page) is your proof. Take a screenshot.

**Step 5: Don't lock in connections yet.** Book hotels and connecting transport only once the booking is 24-48 hours old and the airline hasn't acted.

A useful tip: book directly on the airline's website when possible. Online travel agencies often cancel first, even when the airline might have honored the booking.

## What happens when an airline cancels an error fare?

There is no EU-wide obligation for airlines to honor error fares. The [IATA Resolution 824](https://www.iata.org) and national regulations vary.

In the EU: if an airline cancels a booking, it must refund the full purchase price. No additional compensation. Your separately booked hotels remain your risk.

That said, there are cases where airlines have honored error fares, even days later. This tends to happen when many people booked the deal. The airline avoids public backlash and keeps the bookings.

Some airlines have a track record of honoring mistakes: Finnair, Japan Airlines, and some US carriers have acknowledged error fares as valid in the past. Others, like Ryanair or easyJet, cancel reliably.

Flexible travel dates help enormously. If you were planning to travel anyway and the fare fits, the risk is low. If you need to book extra leave and non-refundable hotels, calculate the total risk beforehand.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### What exactly is an error fare?
An error fare is a flight price that appears significantly below market rate due to a technical glitch, currency conversion error, or human input mistake when airlines load pricing into booking systems. These fares are technically bookable but can be cancelled by the airline.

### Where do error fares show up first?
The most reliable sources are Secret Flying, Scott's Cheap Flights (Going.com), and the Reddit community r/flights. For the fastest notifications, Telegram groups and Discord servers from flight deal communities are the best option.

### How long do error fares stay available?
Typically between 30 minutes and a few hours. Some persist overnight if the airline doesn't correct the system immediately. The more widely shared the deal, the faster it gets removed.

### What happens if the airline cancels my error fare booking?
You get a full refund of the purchase price. Costs for separately booked hotels or connecting transport are not refunded. In the EU, there is no legal right to additional compensation when an airline cancels due to a pricing error.
