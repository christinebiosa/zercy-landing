70% of travelers now use AI during trip planning. But only 38% have actually booked through an AI tool. That gap tells a story. Interest is there. Full trust? Not yet.

We've already covered [how AI is changing travel planning](/en/blog/ai-changing-travel-planning). This article goes further. Not the technology itself, but the decision: AI trip planner or traditional travel agent? Who wins where?

## Where AI clearly wins

**Speed.** An AI tool scans hundreds of flight connections, layover options, and price histories in seconds. At a travel agency, the same result takes 20 minutes. Minimum.

**Price comparison without bias.** Travel agents work on commission. That's no secret and no accusation. But it affects which options land on the table. An AI planner has no incentive to push a specific airline or hotel chain. It shows what fits your budget.

**Complex routing.** Multi-city trips with open-jaw flights, stopovers, and different departure airports: this is where AI shines. It calculates combinations a human would never work through manually. If you want to know [when to book for the best price](/en/blog/when-to-book-flights), AI gives you data-backed answers instead of gut feelings.

**Availability.** Sunday at 11 PM you realize you need to fly the day after tomorrow? No travel agency is open. AI is. Around the clock, no hold music.

**Real-time pricing.** Airfares change by the minute. AI tools can pull live data and tell you whether a price is good or inflated right now. An agent at a travel agency works with whatever their booking system displays at that moment.

## Where travel agents are better

**Luxury travel and honeymoons.** A three-week honeymoon through Southeast Asia with private transfers, spa upgrades, and candlelit beach dinners? That's not a price comparison problem. That's curation. Experienced travel consultants know the hotels personally. They know which room has the best view. No algorithm can replicate that.

**When things go wrong.** Flight canceled, hotel overbooked, luggage lost. In those moments, a human who picks up the phone for you matters. Travel agencies have direct lines to tour operators and airlines. They can solve problems that would leave you stuck in a call center queue for hours.

**Group travel.** 14 people, three different departure cities, varying budgets, one shared destination. That's coordination work requiring patience, experience, and personal communication. A travel consultant can mediate between competing wishes. AI delivers options, but it doesn't negotiate with your mother-in-law about the hotel.

**Insurance advice.** Trip cancellation insurance, international health coverage, rental car waivers: [according to Lonely Planet](https://www.lonelyplanet.com/articles/top-tips-for-budget-travel), one of the areas travelers misjudge most frequently. A good consultant knows the pitfalls and recommends the right package. AI can compare policies, but individual risk assessment remains a human strength.

## Where it's a tie

**Inspiration.** Both can suggest destinations you hadn't considered. An experienced consultant draws on personal experience. AI pulls from thousands of travel reports and reviews. Different knowledge, similar outcome.

**Standard bookings.** Round trip to Mallorca, family hotel, all-inclusive. You don't need AI or an agent for that. Any booking portal handles it in five minutes. [The Points Guy](https://thepointsguy.com/travel/tpg-trends-ai-transforming-future-of-travel/) confirms: for simple bookings, the channel barely matters.

## The third option: AI with real expertise

The most interesting development is happening between the two extremes. AI tools that don't just compare prices but genuinely understand travel. That ask questions before searching. That handle multi-city routing and explain why a stopover in Istanbul is cheaper than a direct flight.

[Google describes](https://thepointsguy.com/travel/tpg-trends-ai-transforming-future-of-travel/) this shift as moving from search to conversation. Instead of filling out forms, you describe what you want. And get answers, not lists.

That's the approach [Zercy](/en/blog/what-is-zercy) takes. Not as a replacement for travel agencies. But as an intelligent alternative for people who want to plan their own trips while expecting the depth of a real consultant. [Condé Nast Traveler](https://www.cntraveler.com/story/best-travel-apps) sees the future right here: AI that makes the planning process more human, not more technical.

## What to take away

There's no universal answer. But there's a simple rule of thumb.

If you want speed, flexibility, and data-driven decisions: AI. If you need personal attention, crisis management, and curated luxury experiences: travel agent. And if you want to [find cheap flights](/en/blog/cheap-flights-tips) without spending hours comparing tabs: an AI tool that analyzes airfares in real time.

The trust gap is shrinking. Every month, more people book through AI. Not because the technology is perfect. But because it learns faster than any other option.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### What can an AI trip planner do better than a travel agent?

AI is faster at price comparison, calculates complex multi-city routes in seconds, and works around the clock. There's also no commission bias. You get results that match your budget, not an agent's revenue model.

### When should I use a travel agent instead of AI?

For luxury trips, honeymoons, and group travel with many participants, an experienced consultant is often the better choice. During the trip itself, when flights get canceled or hotels are overbooked, a travel agency can intervene faster through their direct supplier contacts.

### How trustworthy are AI tools for booking travel?

70% of travelers use AI for planning, and 38% have already booked through it. The gap shows trust is growing but isn't fully there yet. Good AI tools work with real-time data and are transparent about their sources.

### Which types of trips work best with an AI planner?

Flexible multi-city itineraries, budget-conscious city breaks, and complex route planning across different departure airports. Wherever many variables need comparing, AI has the biggest advantage over manual search or a single agent's knowledge.
