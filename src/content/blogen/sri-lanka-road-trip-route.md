Sri Lanka is an island that resists being summarized in a week. Too many contrasts packed too close together. Dry plains and elephants in the north. Cool mountains and tea terraces in the center. Colonial forts and surfers in the south. The best beaches in the east. A good road trip connects cultural heritage, nature, and coastline on a single route you can complete without a domestic flight. That's the rare strength of this island.

Two weeks is enough for a proper Sri Lanka loop. Ten days covers the core stops. Here's the plan.

## What Are the Essential Stops on a Sri Lanka Route?

**Colombo (1–2 days):** The capital isn't the highlight, but it's a solid entry point. The Pettah market quarter is a trading maze that's most intense early in the morning. The Galle Face Green at sunset, street food and sea breeze. Most travelers push inland by day two.

**Kandy (2–3 days):** The heart of Sinhalese culture. The Temple of the Tooth Relic (Sri Dalada Maligawa) is the most sacred Buddhist site on the island. The tooth of the Buddha is kept here. Evening ceremonies with drumming and chanting are genuinely moving. Outside the city, the Peradeniya Botanical Garden is one of the finest in Asia.

**Ella (2–3 days):** Tea plantations, waterfalls, the famous Little Adam's Peak hike, and the Nine Arch Bridge. The train ride from Kandy through the highlands to Ella is widely considered one of the most scenic rail journeys in the world. Book tickets directly at the station or through [Sri Lanka Railways](https://www.railway.gov.lk/web/), not through third-party agents.

**Sigiriya (1–2 days):** The fifth-century rock fortress is the island's most iconic archaeological site. The climb takes 45 minutes. The view justifies every step. Arrive before the tour buses. Combine with the nearby Dambulla Cave Temple.

**Galle (2 days):** This southern colonial city has one of the best-preserved Dutch fortifications in Asia. Narrow streets, cafes, boutiques. A natural unwinding point after a long loop through the interior.

## Which Coast Is Better at Which Time of Year?

Sri Lanka has two coastlines with opposite monsoon patterns. This is the single most important factor in trip planning.

**West coast (Colombo, Galle, Mirissa):** Best from December to March. Dry season, warm water, ideal for swimming and blue whale watching off Mirissa.

**East coast (Trincomalee, Arugam Bay):** Best from April to September. The east is dry when the west is monsoon-affected. Arugam Bay is one of the world's best surf breaks during this window.

If you have less than two weeks, pick one coast. Trying to rush both rarely does either justice.

## How Do You Get Around Sri Lanka?

The rail network is romantic but slow. The Colombo-Kandy train and the Kandy-Ella highland route are must-rides. For the rest of the island, especially off the main lines, tuk-tuks and private drivers give you far more flexibility.

Negotiate tuk-tuk prices for short distances. For full day trips or city-to-city transfers, a private driver costs 30 to 60 euros a day and often comes with solid local knowledge.

Rental cars are technically possible, but traffic is chaotic and you drive on the left. Without previous experience in South Asia, it's not recommended.

The **ETA (Electronic Travel Authority)** is applied for online at [eta.gov.lk](https://www.eta.gov.lk/). Cost: around 50 USD, valid for stays up to 30 days.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### What Does a Sri Lanka Trip Cost?

Budget travelers can manage on 35 to 50 euros per day (guesthouse, local food, tuk-tuk). Mid-range comfort runs 70 to 120 euros per day. Including flights from Europe (500 to 900 euros return), a two-week trip totals roughly 1,500 to 2,500 euros.

### What Vaccinations Are Needed for Sri Lanka?

Hepatitis A and typhoid are recommended. Malaria prophylaxis is worth considering for rural areas in the north and east, depending on your itinerary and travel season. Dengue mosquitoes are more active during rainy season. Mosquito repellent is essential. Get updated medical advice from a travel clinic before departure.

### How Long Does It Take to Reach the Nine Arch Bridge in Ella?

The Nine Arch Bridge is a 20 to 30-minute walk from Ella Town center. The best vantage point is from the hillside above the bridge when a train passes. Trains run several times daily. Check Google Maps or the station for current schedules. Early morning or late afternoon light is best for photos.

### When Should You Climb Sigiriya Rock?

Opening hours are 7am to 5:30pm. Early morning (from 7am) is the only sensible time: cooler temperatures, fewer visitors, and better light for photos. Tour groups arrive around midday and the pathways get crowded. Bring water, sun protection, and solid shoes for the steep metal staircases.
