Los Angeles doesn't make a great first impression.

Long flight. Massive airport. Traffic on the way to the hotel. Everything feels spread out, overwhelming, impossible to explore on foot. Where do you even start?

And then it happens. A sunset over the Pacific. Tacos at midnight at a place with no name. A walk through Silver Lake that suddenly feels like New York. The lights of L.A. from the Hollywood Hills.

And you get it.

## The city of neighborhoods

Los Angeles isn't one city. It's twenty cities sharing a name.

**Santa Monica** is boardwalk, farmers market, evening jogs along the Pacific. Touristy, but the nice kind of touristy.

**Venice Beach** is chaotic, colorful, loud. Skaters, musicians, bodybuilders at Muscle Beach, graffiti walls. Nowhere else shows you L.A. so unfiltered.

**Silver Lake and Los Feliz** are the creative heart of the city. Indie cafés, vintage shops, restaurants you need to book weeks in advance. Musicians, writers, directors live here.

**Beverly Hills** is exactly like in the movies. Wide roads, palm trees, cars that cost more than some apartments. Walk Rodeo Drive once — you have to do it.

**Koreatown** is 24/7. BBQ at 2 AM, karaoke bars, bakeries that never close. One of the most alive corners of the city.

## What you can't miss

**Griffith Observatory** is free, and the view over L.A. is priceless. Best in the evening when the city lights start to glow.

**The Getty Center** on the hill above Brentwood — free entry, world-class art collection, Richard Meier architecture. One of the most underrated highlights in the city.

**In-N-Out Burger** is no longer a secret. But eating a Double-Double Animal Style is still mandatory.

**Malibu** is an hour's drive up the coast. Surfers, luxury homes directly on the water, Point Dume with views over the Pacific.

## Practical info

- **Direct flights** from major European cities to LAX (approx. 11–12 hours)
- **Without a car, you're stuck.** Or nearly. Uber is expensive, buses technically exist.
- **Minimum 7 days**, ideally 10. Everything takes time here.
- **Best time to visit**: May–October. Winters are mild, but June mornings are often grey (June Gloom).

## Why you always come back

L.A. is a city that never shows you everything.

There's still that restaurant in Silver Lake your friend recommended. You haven't seen the murals in Boyle Heights. You've never made it to the farmers market in Pacific Palisades.

Every visit feels like a glimpse. A great glimpse. But you know there's more.
