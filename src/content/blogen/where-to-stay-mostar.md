The Stari Most, Mostar's Old Bridge, is one of the most recognizable images of the Balkans. Ottoman architecture, turquoise Neretva river, mountain villages in the background. But Mostar is also a city with a layered history, having jumped directly from the 1990s war into 21st-century tourism. That tension is part of what makes it compelling.

For travelers, this means: Stari Grad is touristic and pricier. The new city is local and affordable. And those who want to really connect with the place look beyond the tourist crowds.

## Which Neighborhood Fits Your Trip?

**Stari Grad (Old Town):** Old Bridge, Ottoman houses, bazaars. For first-timers, short trips, culture.

**Bulevar / New City:** Western part, cafés, local life. For slow travelers, budget-conscious visitors.

**Neretva Riverbank:** Right on the water, panoramic views. For couples, romantics.

**Surroundings (Blagaj, Počitelj):** 15-20 min away, more authentic. For explorers and day-trippers.

## Stari Grad: Where Mostar Shows Its History

The Old Town of Mostar is a UNESCO World Heritage Site. Kujundžiluk (Coppersmiths Street), mosques, Ottoman caravanserais, and of course the Stari Most. Everything within 400 meters. In summer: packed with tourists. In spring and autumn: incredibly beautiful.

**Who stays here:** First-time visitors, short trips (1-2 nights), anyone who wants to see the bridge first thing in the morning and last thing at night.

**Prices per night:** Guesthouses 40-80 EUR, boutique hotels 80-160 EUR, better hotels 130-230 EUR.

**Top picks:** Kriva Cuprija (romantic, next to the small bridge, Old Town charm), Hotel Stari Most (panoramic views of the bridge), Muslibegovic House (historic Ottoman mansion, a unique experience). ## New City / Bulevar: Local Mostar

The Bulevar divides the Bosniak east from the Croatian west side of the city. West of it: cafés where locals actually sit, cheaper restaurants, almost no souvenir shops. Noticeably more authentic but also less atmospheric.

**Who stays here:** Budget travelers, slow travelers, those who want Mostar beyond the tourist flow.

**Prices:** Guesthouses 25-60 EUR, mid-range 55-100 EUR.

**Top picks:** Villa Fortuna (best value in Mostar), Hotel Pellegrino (west side, modern), private apartments.

## When Is the Best Time to Visit Mostar?

Mostar is a summer destination, but with important caveats. July and August are best for swimming in the Neretva, but the city gets packed. Day-trippers from Dubrovnik and Split flood the Old Town between 10am and 4pm.

**May and June, September and October:** Ideal months. 20-28°C, all sights open, atmosphere far more pleasant without the masses.

**Winter:** Mostar is beautiful and empty in winter. Cold (0-10°C), but affordable and authentic. Fewer hotels open.

## Neretva Riverbank: Romance on the Turquoise River

Hotels directly on the Neretva have the best views: turquoise water, cliffs, bridges. Few options, usually booked out. Book early.

**Who stays here:** Couples, honeymooners, photographers.

**Prices:** 90-200 EUR per night.

**Top picks:** Hotel Mostar (Neretva views), Villa Nur (historic house on the river), boutique guesthouses on the bank.

Mostar is also a perfect day-trip base. Blagaj (dervish monastery built into a cliff), Počitelj (Ottoman hilltop village), and Kravice Waterfalls: all within 20-30 minutes.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Which neighborhood is best for staying in Mostar?

For atmosphere: Stari Grad (Old Town), right next to the Stari Most. For budget and local life: Bulevar/New City. For romance: hotels on the Neretva bank. If you only have one night: Old Town.

### When should you visit Mostar?

May, June and September are ideal. Pleasant temperatures, no peak-season crowds. July-August is great for swimming but very touristy. Winter is affordable and empty.

### How safe is Mostar for travelers?

Mostar is well-suited for tourists. Pickpocketing is the main risk in the Old Town in summer. War memorial sites like the War Museum provide important context but pose no safety concern.

### How much does a night in Mostar cost?

Old Town guesthouses: 40-80 EUR. Boutique hotels: 80-160 EUR. New City/budget: 25-55 EUR. Mostar is significantly cheaper than Dubrovnik or Split at a comparable charm level.
