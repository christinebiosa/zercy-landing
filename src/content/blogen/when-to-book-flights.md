There's one question every traveler asks at some point: when is the cheapest time to book?

The honest answer: it depends. But not on luck — on a few concrete factors.

## The ground rules by route type

### Short-haul Europe
**Optimal window: 6–10 weeks before departure**

European low-cost carriers like Ryanair, easyJet, and Wizz Air fill their planes fast. Booking 3 months out often costs the same as 3 weeks out — simply because demand is spread fairly evenly.

Exception: school holidays. Easter and summer flights from major hubs fill up early. Here, earlier is better.

### Transatlantic (USA, Canada, Caribbean)
**Optimal window: 3–5 months before departure**

Transatlantic routes are booked well in advance. Airlines open booking windows 11–12 months ahead — the cheapest tickets disappear in the first few weeks.

If you're thinking about a spontaneous trip to New York: last-minute rarely works here.

### Asia (Japan, Thailand, Singapore, Bali)
**Optimal window: 4–6 months before departure**

Asia flights are long and comparatively expensive. Airlines expect long-term planning. Booking early gets you better seats at lower prices.

Important: check visa requirements early. Japan, for example, requires no visa for EU citizens, but other destinations do.

### Middle East and Africa
**Optimal window: 2–4 months before departure**

Price variance is highest here. Dubai flights can range from €300 to €900 depending on airline, booking timing, and season. Market-watching pays off.

## What else affects the price

**Day of the week for departure**
Friday and Sunday are expensive. Tuesday and Wednesday are cheap. This applies to almost every route.

**Time of day**
Early morning and late evening flights are less popular — and cheaper. 6 AM flights put people off. Use that to your advantage.

**Seasonality**
Every destination has a high season. Mallorca in August costs more than Mallorca in October — but October is often nicer anyway.

## Last-minute: when it works

Last-minute only makes sense when:
- The route has lots of competition (e.g. London–Barcelona)
- You're truly flexible — including with the destination
- You're not tied to specific times

For family trips or pre-booked hotels: forget last-minute. The potential price advantage is erased by the risk.

---

The short summary: book short-haul 6–10 weeks out, long-haul 3–5 months out. Avoid Friday and Sunday departures. And be flexible — a single free travel day can be worth a hundred euros.
