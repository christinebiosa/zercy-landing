The Bay of Kotor is not a typical destination. The water cuts deep into the mountains, the peaks rise almost vertically from the sea, and in the middle of it all sits a Venetian old town locked behind UNESCO-listed walls. Montenegro was a well-kept secret for years. That chapter is over. Kotor has arrived.

What does that mean for your accommodation? The neighborhood you pick matters more here than in most cities. Old town atmosphere or quiet northern shore? Marina luxury or local ferry to a cheaper village? Here is the honest breakdown.

## Which Neighborhood Fits Your Trip?

**Stari Grad (Old Town):** UNESCO World Heritage Site, Venetian architecture, medieval walls. For first-timers and everyone who wants to feel completely immersed.

**Dobrota:** Quiet village north of the old town, right on the water, low tourist density. For couples and anyone who wants sleep, sea views, and no crowds.

**Prčanj:** Small village on the opposite shore, historic churches, dramatic panoramic views. For slow travelers and a change from old-town intensity.

**Muo:** Local fishing village, 5 minutes by ferry from the old town, lowest prices around. For budget travelers without giving up location.

**Tivat/Porto Montenegro:** Modern luxury marina, yachts, design hotels, 15 minutes by car. For those who want beach, pool, and premium service.

## Stari Grad (Old Town): Sleeping Inside Venetian Walls

Kotor's old town is fully enclosed by medieval walls. Squares, fountains, cathedrals, narrow lanes and cats everywhere. The [official Montenegro tourism site](https://www.visit-montenegro.com) describes Kotor as one of the best-preserved medieval townscapes on the Adriatic. That is not an exaggeration.

Inside the walls there are a handful of hotels and guesthouses, plus many apartments on Booking.com. Staying here means everything is walkable: restaurants, cafes, the fortress staircase, the harbor.

**Who fits here:** First-timers, culture travelers, couples who value atmosphere over space.

**Nightly rates:** Budget 70-120 EUR (apartments), boutique 130-250 EUR.

**Top picks:** Old Town Palace Hotel (small boutique property in the lanes), Hippocampus Hotel (historic house on the square), various apartments directly beside the city walls. ## Dobrota: Quiet Shore with Bay Views

Dobrota starts immediately north of the old town and stretches as a long coastal village along the bay. No souvenir shops, no tour groups. Instead: waterfront promenades, old stone mansions, small seafood restaurants, and that view of still water backed by mountains.

Hotels and villas in Dobrota are often in restored Venetian buildings. Many have direct waterfront terraces or small piers. The walk to the old town takes 10-15 minutes.

**Who fits here:** Couples, anyone seeking quiet, those who prioritize sea views and calm over central location.

**Nightly rates:** Mid-range 120-220 EUR, premium 200-380 EUR.

**Top picks:** Hotel Forza Leo (small waterfront hotel with balcony views of the bay), Cattaro Design Hotel (modern design, waterfront terrace), various villas with private waterfront access.

## When Is the Best Time to Visit Kotor?

May, June, and September are ideal. Warm, clear water, far fewer people than peak summer. July and August: cruise ships arrive daily, the old town fills wall to wall, and prices spike hard. April and October: cheaper, quiet, and good temperatures for hiking the fortress walls.

Anyone planning to climb the fortress (over 1,300 steps up to Castel San Giovanni) should go early morning or late evening. At midday in summer it is a punishing heat climb.

## Prčanj: Quiet Side of the Bay with Panoramic Views

Prčanj is on the opposite shore of the bay, a 15-20 minute drive around the bay or a short trip on the small passenger ferry. The village is known for its panoramic view: from here you see Kotor from the other side, the city walls, and Monte San Giovanni rising behind.

Accommodation options are smaller and more personal here, often family-run guesthouses. Waterfront restaurants, an authentic pace, and none of the tourist concentration of the old town.

**Who fits here:** Slow travelers, photographers, anyone who wants to see Kotor from the quiet side.

**Nightly rates:** Budget 60-100 EUR, mid-range 90-160 EUR.

**Top picks:** Waterfront B&Bs and guesthouses, several holiday apartments with panoramic terraces.

## Muo: Budget-Friendly, Local, 5 Minutes by Ferry

Muo is a small fishing village directly across the bay, visible from Kotor's old town. A passenger ferry connects the two in under 5 minutes. That makes Muo an affordable base without sacrificing location.

Local residents, no cruise tourists, genuine konobas. Prices are noticeably lower than in the old town. The ferry runs regularly, including evenings.

**Who fits here:** Budget travelers, backpackers, anyone who wants good value without compromise on proximity.

**Nightly rates:** Budget 45-80 EUR, mid-range 75-130 EUR.

**Top picks:** Apartments in private accommodations, small guesthouses with terrace views over the bay.

## Tivat/Porto Montenegro: Marina Luxury and Yachts

Porto Montenegro is a superyacht marina 15 kilometers from Kotor. Luxury hotels, design restaurants, boutiques, infinity pools. For travelers who want the Adriatic feeling with a beach club, Kotor itself is a short taxi ride away.

The marina was developed on a former military shipyard and is now one of the most exclusive destinations on the Adriatic.

**Who fits here:** Luxury travelers, couples who want beach alongside culture, anyone putting marina atmosphere above old-town immersion.

**Nightly rates:** Mid-range 200-400 EUR, premium 400-900 EUR.

**Top picks:** Regent Porto Montenegro (the marina's flagship hotel), The Chedi Lustica Bay (luxury resort slightly outside the marina), various apartments inside the marina complex for lower price points.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Which neighborhood is best for a first visit to Kotor?

The old town (Stari Grad) is essential for a first visit. At least one night inside the walls, climbing the fortress stairs early in the morning, sitting on Piazza of Arms in the evening. Anyone staying more than 3 nights can switch to Dobrota for the second part.

### How expensive are hotels in Kotor in summer?

July and August are peak season: boutique hotels from 150 EUR, mid-range 100-160 EUR, apartments from 70 EUR. May, June, and September are noticeably cheaper. Note: cruise ships arrive daily during peak months, which floods the old town during daytime hours.

### How do you get from Dubrovnik to Kotor?

By bus: several daily connections, journey time around 2 to 2.5 hours, price 10-15 EUR. By rental car: same route, more flexibility, with the option to stop along the coast. The bus connection is practical and reliable.

### What should you not miss in Kotor?

Climbing the fortress wall to Castel San Giovanni (go early morning), experiencing the old town before cruise ships arrive (before 9am or after 6pm), taking the ferry across to Muo, and a boat trip through the bay. And counting the cats.
