The best moment of any trip is standing somewhere thinking: how does nobody know about this yet?

These places still exist. You just have to look a little further.

## 1. Matera, Southern Italy

Matera is one of the oldest continuously inhabited cities in the world. Cave houses carved directly into rock. Alleyways that feel like a labyrinth. European Capital of Culture in 2019.

And still: not overrun. Not yet.

Getting there: fly to Bari or Naples, then rent a car (about 1.5–2 hours).

## 2. Kotor, Montenegro

The Bay of Kotor looks like a fjord but is Mediterranean. Venetian old town, medieval city walls, mountains right behind it. Cheaper than Croatia, more beautiful than many better-known destinations.

Flights to Tivat (10 minutes away) or Dubrovnik (1.5 hours).

## 3. Plovdiv, Bulgaria

Plovdiv is Europe's oldest continuously inhabited city — older than Athens, older than Rome. The old town sits on three hills. The nightlife is young, cheap, and surprisingly good.

Fly to Sofia, then 2 hours by bus or train.

## 4. Sintra, Portugal (Off-Season)

Sintra is well-known by now. In July and August it's packed. In October, November, March? Almost empty, no queues for tickets, hotel prices half as much.

Same destination. Completely different experience.

## 5. Ohrid, North Macedonia

A UNESCO lake, an old town full of Byzantine churches, mountains in the background. Less than 4 flight hours from Germany. Barely any mass tourism. Fresh fish by the lake for €8.

## 6. Évora, Portugal

Less than 1.5 hours from Lisbon, but a different world. A Roman temple in the middle of the city, a 16th-century bone chapel, the Alentejo wine region right outside the door.

Most Lisbon visitors never make it here. Their loss.

## 7. Tbilisi, Georgia

Tbilisi is the secret that's just now getting out. Old town with wooden balcony houses, baths with sulfur thermal water, natural wine (invented here), and mountains in the background.

Fly from Frankfurt or Vienna. Around 4 hours. Often cheap.
