Business class isn't for everyone, every route, or every occasion. But dismissing it as luxury for other people sometimes means missing the smartest decision.

## When Business Class Makes Sense

**Flights over 8 hours**
Under 4 hours: economy is fine. Between 4 and 8: depends on price. Over 8 hours: the bed, the sleep, the ability to function on arrival — that has real value.

Someone stepping off a flight in New York at 7am who needs to be in a meeting by 9 thinks about business class differently than a leisure traveler.

**Special occasions**
Honeymoon. Milestone birthday. Once-in-a-lifetime trip to Japan. Some trips deserve the better version.

**When the price difference is small**
This happens more often than you'd think — especially with last-minute upgrades, award miles, or bookings with cashback credit cards.

## What You Get in Business Class

Varies a lot by airline and route. But the standard on long-haul today:

- **Flat bed**: most intercontinental flights offer lie-flat seats
- **Food**: multiple courses, usually good
- **Lounge access**: before the flight and during connections
- **Priority boarding**: no crowding
- **Baggage**: more allowance, priority delivery

## How to Get Business Class Cheaper

**Miles and points**
Miles & More, Flying Blue, British Airways Avios — frequent flyers who actually use their miles can book long-haul business class well below list price.

**Gate upgrades**
Ask just before boarding if an upgrade is available. Sometimes they're €200–400 on routes where the list price is €3,000.

**Bidding systems**
Many airlines (Lufthansa, Swiss, KLM) run upgrade auctions. You place a bid — the minimum is often surprisingly low.

## When Economy Is the Better Choice

- Short-haul under 4 hours
- When your budget is tight and you'd rather travel more
- If you can sleep anywhere regardless
- When the price difference is 10×
