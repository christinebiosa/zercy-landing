One hour with a mountain gorilla family in the rainforest. No fence. No cage. No glass. A silverback looking back at you. This is not a zoo experience. It's something different entirely.

Rwanda is the gateway to mountain gorillas. This guide tells you what it really costs, how to get the permit, and what else to expect.

## What makes gorilla trekking in Rwanda special?

There are around 1,000 mountain gorillas left on Earth. They live in a small shared zone: Rwanda, Uganda and the Democratic Republic of Congo. Most travelers choose Rwanda for one reason: safety, infrastructure and a well-organized tourism sector.

**Volcanoes National Park** (Parc National des Volcans) sits in northwest Rwanda, about 2 hours from Kigali. It's part of the Virunga Massif, a volcanic chain at 2,500 to 4,500 meters elevation. Ten habituated gorilla families can be visited. One group of 8 tourists per family per day.

Trekking duration: between 1 and 7 hours depending on where the gorilla family has moved to. It's hiking in mountain rainforest with inclines and sometimes heavy rain. You don't need to be a serious hiker, but basic fitness is required.

Permits are booked officially through the [Rwanda Development Board](https://www.rdb.rw). Cost: $1,500 USD per person (2026). They're limited: 8 people per gorilla family, 10 families, equals 80 permits per day for the whole park.

## How do you get a gorilla permit for Rwanda?

Directly through the [RDB website](https://www.rdb.rw) or through licensed tour operators. Credit card accepted. No middleman necessary, but reputable operators can help if the online booking process hits issues.

How far in advance? For high season (June to September, December to February): book 3 to 6 months ahead. Popular months sell out weeks after availability opens. In low season (March to May, October to November) last-minute permits are sometimes available.

Cancellation policy: 25 percent fee up to 30 days before the date. No refund after that.

The permit is tied to the individual. Reselling is not allowed.

## What does a Rwanda gorilla trip cost in total?

The permit at $1,500 is the biggest single cost.

**Accommodation**: Lodges near Volcanoes National Park (Kinigi, Musanze) range from budget guesthouses ($40 to $80) to luxury lodges right at the park boundary ($500 to $800 per night). The famous "gorilla hotels" offer direct views of the volcanic chain. If the budget allows: it's a remarkable experience.

**Flights to Kigali**: From Europe from $500 to $700. Kigali International Airport is well connected (Brussels Airlines, RwandAir, Turkish Airlines, Ethiopian Airlines).

**Kigali itself**: Rwanda's capital is consistently ranked among Africa's cleanest and safest cities. The Kigali Genocide Memorial is an emotional, necessary visit. It explains how Rwanda became what it is today after the 1994 genocide.

**Total budget for 5 days**: permit ($1,500), mid-range accommodation ($300 to $600), flight, transfers, food. Minimum $2,500 to $3,500 per person.

## What else is there to do in Rwanda beyond gorilla trekking?

**Nyungwe Forest National Park** in the southwest: one of Africa's oldest rainforests. Chimpanzee trekking (permit $100), colobus monkeys, forest trails. The Canopy Walkway (a suspension bridge through the tree canopy, 70 meters high) is unique in Africa.

**Lake Kivu** on the western border: one of Africa's most beautiful lakes. Hills, tea plantations, fishing boats. Gisenyi and Kibuye are relaxed stops.

**Golden Monkey Trekking**: Volcanoes National Park also has golden monkeys. Permit costs $100. Less popular than gorilla trekking but fascinating and easier to book.

**Musanze Caves**: lava caves from a volcanic eruption. Guided tours, inexpensive, interesting as a side trip.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### How much does a gorilla trekking permit cost in Rwanda?

The gorilla permit for Volcanoes National Park costs $1,500 USD per person (2026 rate). It's booked officially through the Rwanda Development Board at rdb.rw. The permit grants one hour with a habituated gorilla family in the mountain rainforest.

### When is the best time for gorilla trekking in Rwanda?

Two main seasons: June to September (dry, best trekking conditions) and December to February (short dry season). During rainy seasons (March to May and October to November) trekking is harder with muddy trails but permits are more available and the forests are lush green.

### How fit do you need to be for gorilla trekking?

Basic hiking fitness is sufficient. Trekking can last 1 to 7 hours with inclines at 2,500 to over 3,000 meters elevation. Sturdy footwear and trekking poles are recommended. Guides help navigate through dense undergrowth. People with significant physical limitations should contact RDB beforehand.

### How far in advance do you need to book a gorilla permit?

In high season (June to September) book 3 to 6 months ahead. In low season (October to November, March to May) shorter advance booking is often possible. For December and the Christmas period: 4 to 5 months in advance recommended.
