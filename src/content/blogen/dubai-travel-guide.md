Dubai is the most misunderstood city in the world. Some expect a fake plastic world. Others arrive with inflated luxury fantasies. The reality sits somewhere in between and surprises almost every first-time visitor.

Dubai is a real city with 3.5 million residents, 90 percent of them expats from over 200 countries. That creates a cosmopolitan energy unlike anything else. At the same time, Dubai only works as a travel destination when you know what you're actually looking for.

## When is the best time to visit Dubai?

Dubai has an extreme climate. November through March is perfect travel time: 68-82 degrees Fahrenheit, no rain, pleasant evenings. This is also peak season with the highest prices.

April and October are shoulder months: still good to warm, lower prices.

May through September: temperatures hit 100-118 degrees Fahrenheit daily. Humidity from July through September is brutal. Outdoor activities between 10am and 4pm are essentially impossible. If you travel in summer: it's all about indoor Dubai (malls, water parks, indoor activities). Flights and hotels are dramatically cheaper.

For Europeans escaping winter, January/February is the sweet spot: below peak pricing, but perfect weather.

## Which neighborhoods are actually worth your time?

Dubai isn't a compact city. Distances are large, public transport limited to metro and bus. Taxis and Careem (local Uber equivalent) are cheap and unavoidable.

**Downtown Dubai:** Burj Khalifa, Dubai Mall, Dubai Fountain. This is postcard Dubai. Touristy, but for good reason: the density of experiences is unbeatable. Stay here if convenience and location matter more than price.

**Dubai Marina:** Cosmopolitan, waterfront, lively beach strip along JBR Promenade. Many international restaurants, nightlife, good hotel variety. Right for younger travelers and couples.

**Deira and Bur Dubai (Old Town):** The old, real Dubai before the skyscrapers. Gold Souk, Spice Souk, Al Fahidi Historical District. This is where you understand why Dubai is more than towers. Cheaper hotels, local restaurants, traditional abra ferry across the Creek.

**Palm Jumeirah:** Luxury resorts, Atlantis The Palm, private beach. Right for honeymoon travelers or a one-time five-star experience.

The [Dubai Tourism Department](https://www.visitdubai.com/en) has a good overview of current events and neighborhood guides.

## What does Dubai actually cost?

Dubai is expensive, but not brutally so if you know where to look.

**Flights:** Direct flights Frankfurt/Vienna/Zurich to Dubai with Emirates or Lufthansa: $380-750 return. Cheapest time: May through September (summer heat discount, up to 50 percent off).

**Hotels:** 3-star in Dubai Marina: $75-130. 4-star Downtown: $140-270. 5-star beach resort: $320-850. Off-season (summer): roughly half of all that.

**Food:** Food courts in malls and street food: $9-16. Mid-range restaurant: $27-48. Alcohol in restaurants: $13-22 per drink (only in licensed establishments, not everywhere).

**Burj Khalifa:** Tickets from $38 (floor 124) to $130 (floor 148, sunset slot). Online booking significantly cheaper than at the door.

Alcohol is legal in Dubai for non-Muslims in licensed hotels and restaurants. In public areas and the old city, discretion is appropriate.

## Which activities are worth it and which are overrated?

**Worth it:**
- Burj Khalifa sunset slot (book online weeks ahead)
- Dubai Creek tour by abra (traditional wooden boat, $0.27)
- Desert safari with sandboarding (half-day tour around $85, unforgettable)
- Dubai Mall: even if shopping isn't your thing. Largest aquarium in the world, ice rink, 1,200 shops.

**Overrated:**
- Burj Al Arab interior (afternoon tea costs $165+, the exterior view is more impressive anyway)
- Miracle Garden in summer (closes April-October, the garden only works in the main season)

## Frequently Asked Questions

### How many days do you need in Dubai?

Minimum 4 days for the core experiences. Ideally 5-6 days if you want a desert safari and the contrast between old town and new city. With fewer than 3 nights, you spend more time in transit than exploring.

### What are the dress code rules in Dubai?

In hotels, malls, and restaurants there are no strict requirements. In public spaces, souks, and mosques: covered shoulders and knees. Swimwear only at the beach and pool. In the old city (Al Fahidi, souks) respecting local norms is important.

### How safe is Dubai for solo travelers?

Dubai ranks among the safest travel destinations in the world. Crime rate is extremely low. Solo female travelers report minimal harassment. Important: know local laws. Public displays of affection are officially prohibited. Alcohol only in licensed areas.

### Which credit card is better in Dubai?

Visa and Mastercard are accepted everywhere. Cash (AED, Dirham) only needed for souks and smaller shops. ATM withdrawals work reliably. Currency exchange offices near Dubai Creek often offer better rates than hotels.
