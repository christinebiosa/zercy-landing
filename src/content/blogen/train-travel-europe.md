There are routes in Europe where the train isn't the second-best option. It's the best.

Faster city center to city center. No airport chaos. Scenery instead of clouds. And the CO₂ footprint? A fraction of flying.

## 5 European Train Routes Worth Taking

### 1. Paris — Barcelona (High Speed, 6.5 hours)

The TGV between Paris Gare de Lyon and Barcelona Sants is one of Europe's most comfortable rail connections. No stop, high speed through the Pyrenees.

Time advantage over flying when you factor in travel to the airport, security, and waiting time: often equal or faster by train.

### 2. Zürich — Milan (Gotthard Tunnel, 3.5 hours)

Since the Gotthard Base Tunnel opened in 2016 — the world's longest railway tunnel — Zürich to Milan takes under four hours. The tunnel crossing itself is an experience.

### 3. Vienna — Salzburg — Munich (Railjet, 4 hours)

Railjet and ICE connect these three cities several times daily. All three stations are central. Flying makes no sense here for anyone.

### 4. Amsterdam — London (Eurostar, 4 hours)

Under the English Channel. Board in Amsterdam, step off at London St. Pancras. No airport, no Heathrow security queue.

### 5. Budapest — Prague — Berlin (Night Train)

The night train from Budapest via Vienna, Prague to Berlin — a classic reborn. Board in the evening, wake up at your destination. One hotel and one transport in one.

## When the Train Doesn't Work

Routes over 8 hours without a night train option. Cross-connections in Eastern Europe without direct services. When the flight price is so low it beats the time difference.

But for city trips within Europe? Check the train first, then the flight.
